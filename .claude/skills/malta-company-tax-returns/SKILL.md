# Malta Company Tax Returns

## Overview

Expert reference for preparing Malta company income tax returns with zero errors. Grounded in the full text of the Income Tax Act (Cap. 123, as amended to Act III of 2026) plus MTCA guidance and practitioner sources verified July 2026 (YA 2025 / YA 2026).

**Core discipline: never state a figure, rate, fraction, deadline, or article number from memory alone — verify it in the reference files below before it goes into a return or advice.** An unverified number in a tax return is an error waiting for an assessment.

## Quick reference (verified for YA 2025 / YA 2026)

- **Rate**: 35% (art. 56(6)). Elective **FITWI 15% final, no imputation/refunds** from YA 2025 (L.N. 188/2025; 5-year lock-in; floored at standard-system outcome).
- **Return**: "Income Tax Return and Self-Assessment" — electronic only, filed by a **registered tax practitioner** via MTCA online (form MTCA02 access), based on **audited financial statements**.
- **Deadline**: 9 months after FY end (FY ending Jan–Jun → 31 March following). E-filing extensions published yearly — YA2026: Dec-2025 year-end → manual 30 Sep 2026, electronic **27 Nov 2026**. Extensions do NOT extend payment.
- **Provisional tax**: 30 Apr 20% / 31 Aug 30% / 21 Dec 50% (PT1; reducible by declaration). DDT10 companies: payment deferred to earlier of distribution or **18 months** after FY end (DDT10 valid 3 years — renew).
- **Tax accounts, allocation order**: FTA → IPA → FIA → MTA → UA (UA = residual, only account that can be negative). Refunds attach **only to MTA/FIA** distributions. Dividends: IPA must be distributed before MTA (art. 68(3)).
- **Refunds** (ITMA 48(4)/(4A)): 6/7 default; 5/7 passive interest/royalties (foreign tax <5% and not trade-derived) or anti-abuse-failing PH dividends; 2/3 where any DTR claimed on FIA profits; 100% participating holding (election to tax). Preconditions: shareholder registration, dividend certificate (art. 59(5)), return filed, tax paid; claim within 4 years; refund in the currency of share capital.
- **Participation exemption** (12(1)(u)): equity holding (2 of 3 rights, not property company) + any of 6 tests (≥5% / option / first refusal / board seat / €1,164,000 + 183 days / business furtherance). Dividends need anti-abuse (EU, or ≥15% foreign tax, or ≤50% passive); gains don't. Claimed by NON-inclusion in the return. Exempt PH profits → **UA since YA 2020** (not FTA).
- **NID**: 20-year MGS yield + 5% (YA2025 8.70%; YA2026 9.19% — verify CBM quarter), × risk capital; cap 90% of chargeable income; ALL-shareholder approval; TRA 100; **110% of relieved profits → FTA** (no refund); deemed interest income for shareholders (non-residents usually exempt 12(1)(c)(i)).
- **Fiscal units** (S.L. 123.189): ≥95% (2 of 3), coterminous periods, election window 6 months from day after FY end (not before 1 Aug; Dec YE → 1 Jan–30 Jun); single consolidated return + audited consolidated FS; ~5% upfront in lieu of refunds; 18-month payment only if ALL members have DDT10.
- **Penalties**: late return €50→€1,500 scale (Schedule to Cap. 123); omission 1.5%/month of endangered tax (max 90%); self-correct within 12 months of return date → fully remitted, later pre-enquiry → 0.1%/month, post-enquiry pre-assessment → 0.75%/month. Interest **0.6%/month** on unpaid tax (not remittable). Additional tax is never imputable/refundable.
- **Amendment**: AF1 further return; assessment window 5 years from end of filing year; objection 30 days, undisputed tax paid first.

## Workflow for preparing/reviewing a return

1. **Profile the company**: incorporation (Malta-incorporated = always resident), domicile (worldwide vs remittance basis — foreign capital gains of non-doms never taxable), FY end → deadlines, DDT10 status, fiscal unit membership, FITWI election, Pillar Two group scope.
2. **Segregate final-tax income OUT of the main computation**: art. 5A property transfers (8%/10%/5%/2% on value → FTA, TRA 53), 15% investment income WHT, 15% rental option (31D), tonnage tax. These never enter chargeable income and carry no refund.
3. **Build the computation per taxed-account column (IPA/MTA/FIA)** on return pages 3–5: add-backs (depreciation, provisions, fines, entertainment private element, €14,000 car cap, ILR excess), capital allowances (TRA 5; rates table in computation-guide), balancing statements (art. 24 — mandatory with the return on any disposal), losses (trading: carry-forward against total income; capital: ring-fenced; capital allowances: same-source only), group relief (current-year losses only, coterminous periods, account matching), NID (TRA 100), deductions with conditions (14(5) VAT invoice; 14(3) pre-trading 18 months).
4. **Apply exemptions by NON-inclusion** (participation exemption, foreign PE) — check anti-abuse, anti-hybrid (deductible dividends), non-cooperative jurisdiction exclusion.
5. **Claim DTR in the correct order** (treaty → unilateral → FRFTC; never combined on the same income); FRFTC needs auditor certificate + FIA allocation; remember 2/3 refund consequence.
6. **Allocate distributable profits** FTA→IPA→FIA→MTA→UA including IPA notional allocations (€250/m² deemed rent, property-loan interest) and NID 110% FTA shift; reconcile page 6 to reserves (TIFD 3950). Rule 7: material misallocation = incomplete return.
7. **Attach every required TRA** (see filing-guide index; from YA2026: TRA 135 transfer pricing disclosure, TRA 136 permits/leases) and answer the new page-2 questions (TP, FITWI).
8. **Cross-verify before filing**: every rate/fraction against the references; tax payment by the settlement date (NOT the e-filing extension); dividend certificates and refund registrations in place for planned distributions.

## Common mistakes (all documented — full list of 18 in computation-guide)

| Mistake | Correction |
|---|---|
| Participation-exempt profits allocated to FTA | UA since YA 2020 (L.N. 194/2019) |
| 5/7 refund assumed for all royalties | Only if foreign tax <5% AND not trade-derived; else 6/7 (or 2/3 if DTR claimed) |
| E-filing extension treated as payment extension | Payment due at statutory settlement date |
| Group relief with non-coterminous periods or b/f losses | Current-year losses only; same start AND end dates |
| Capital allowances offset across sources / kept after cessation | Same-source only; lost on cessation |
| NID claimed without all-shareholder approval or above 90% cap | Approval mandatory; excess carries forward |
| Refund claimed before registration/tax paid/dividend certificate | All preconditions first; 4-year limit |
| Missing balancing statement on disposal | Art. 24 statement mandatory with the return |
| Tax Accounts Rules cited as S.L. 123.108 | S.L. 123.101 |
| Answering YA-specific questions (extension dates, NID rate, new TRAs) from memory | These change yearly — verify in filing-guide, and web-check if the YA is newer than 2026 |

## Staleness rule

The references were verified **July 2026** (YA 2025/2026). For any later year of assessment: the extension tables, NID reference rate, TRA list, budget measures, and Pillar Two status MUST be re-verified against mtca.gov.mt / legislation.mt before use. Statutory structure (tax accounts, refund fractions, imputation) changes rarely; administrative figures change every year.
