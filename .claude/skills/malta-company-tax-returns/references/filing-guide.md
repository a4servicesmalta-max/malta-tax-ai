# Malta Company Income Tax Return — Filing Mechanics (YA2025 & YA2026)

## 1. The Electronic Corporate Income Tax Return

### 1.1 Who files, and how
- Return name: **"Income Tax Return and Self-Assessment"** for companies; due within 9 months of financial year end. Source: https://mtca.gov.mt/business-tax/corporate/Income_Tax
- **Company tax returns can only be submitted by a registered Tax Practitioner** through MTCA online services. Access via form **MTCA02** online; practitioner retains a copy signed by a Company Director. Automatic acknowledgement issued.
- The **e-Return** is a spreadsheet application submitting the return **and the financial statements**; personalised per company (pre-populated directors/shareholders per Registrar of Companies, income tax payments, company-specific unique key); built-in verification checks. Access: 2-step authentication via e-ID. Sources: https://mtca.gov.mt/business-tax/corporate/irdservicesonline ; https://www.csbgroup.com/malta-corporate-taxation/tax-return/
- Return based on **audited financial statements**; 35% flat; self-assessment (Commissioner assesses only on disagreement).
- **Fiscal units**: the **principal taxpayer** files a single consolidated return based on consolidated audited financial statements; a **"supplemental document" for Fiscal Units** is downloadable each YA. Fiscal-unit elections/changes within 6 months from the day after FY end, not before 1 August of the FY-end calendar year. Sources: https://mtca.gov.mt/home/2025/06/22/year-of-assessment-2025-tax-return-for-companies ; https://mtca.gov.mt/home/2026/05/07/year-of-assessment-2026-tax-return-for-companies

### 1.2 Return structure (selected known pages)
- **Page 2**: general questions — since YA2025 includes a Transfer Pricing Rules [S.L.123.207] question; since YA2026 also a FITWI Regulations 2025 question.
- **Page 5**: refund/overpayment section — bank details field **removed in YA2026** (bank details via "My Account" on the MTCA portal).
- **Page 7**: Details of Directors — TIN of country of tax residence **mandatory** where residence is in an EU Member State; foreign residential address requested (since YA2023).
- **Part 8 Q25, Part 9 Q4 & Q6**: wording added in YA2026 for the Audit Exemption Rules [S.L.372.33].

## 2. Deadlines

### 2.1 Statutory rule
- Return due within **9 months from FY end**; FY ending 1 Jan–30 Jun file by **31 March of the following year**; FY ending 31 Jul onwards: 9 months after FY end. Source: https://mtca.gov.mt/business-tax/corporate/Income_Tax

### 2.4 Manual vs electronic
- Extensions apply **only to electronic filing of the return, not to tax payments**; manual returns and **all tax payments** by statutory due dates.
- Non-company returns YA2026 (individuals; clubs TA37; religious entities TA36; partnerships TA5; trusts Trust 04): extended to **31 Jul 2026**, tax also due then.

## 3. Provisional Tax, Settlement Tax, Payments

- **Provisional tax (PT)** instalments (based on last filed self-assessment/PT1): **30 April – 20%; 31 August – 30%; 21 December – 50%**.
- PT demanded via **PT1 claim form**; reducible via **PT Reduction Declaration Form** before the payment date (excessive reduction risks fines/interest).
- **Settlement (balance) tax**: due by the **tax settlement date = statutory return date** (NOT extended by the e-filing extension).
- **Deferred payment for DDT10-determination companies** (mostly foreign business/shareholding income): tax payable by the **earlier of profit distribution or 18 months after period end**; DDT10 determinations renewable every 3 years.

## 4. Penalties and Interest (ITMA, CAP 372)

### 4.1 Late filing — additional tax (per return, non-cumulative)
€50 (within 6 months) / €200 (6–12) / €400 (12–18) / €600 (18–24) / €800 (24–36) / €1,000 (36–48) / €1,200 (48–60) / €1,500 (>60, cap).
Lateness measured from the manual submission deadline (the €50 tier triggers if not filed by the extended online deadline). No interest on additional tax.

### 4.2 Omissions / incorrect returns — additional tax on "endangered tax"
- **1.5% per month**, capped at 60 months (max 90%). Voluntary disclosure: before enquiry notice within 12 months of return date → **fully remitted**; before enquiry but later → **0.1%/month**; after enquiry notification but before assessment → **0.75%/month**.
- 2025: new **out-of-court settlement mechanism** — voluntary correction with payment extinguishes criminal liability; penalties €10,000–€1,000,000 ([PwC](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/malta/corporate/tax-administration)).

### 4.3 Interest on late payment
- **0.6% per month or part thereof** for tax payable on/after 31 Aug 2022 (previously 0.33%/month); runs from the tax settlement date to full payment. Cannot be remitted.

## 5. Amending Returns, Assessments, Objections

- **Amendments**: **AF1** ("further return", blank, taxpayer completes) to adjust/correct/add to a filed Return and Self-Assessment; **AF** (Correction Form, pre-printed) to correct an MTCA-issued Tax Statement. Filing does not relieve penalties for the original omission; resulting balance paid **with** the AF/AF1 (interest from the settlement date).
- **Tax Statement**: issued after processing; reflects self-assessed figures; NOT an assessment.
- **Assessments**: Commissioner may assess within **5 years from end of the year in which the return (or further return) is filed**; no limit for non-disclosure/wilful default; collection within 8 years.
- **Objections**: within **30 days** of the Notice of Assessment; grounds stated; **undisputed tax paid first**; unfiled return must accompany objection. Refusal → **Administrative Review Tribunal within 30 days**; then Court of Appeal (points of law).

## 6. YA2025 / YA2026 Changes

### YA2025 (MTCA, 22 Jun 2025)
- Page 2: new **Transfer Pricing Rules [S.L.123.207] question**.
- New TRAs 131–134; TRA 35 updated. Fiscal Unit supplemental document available.
- **FITWI**: elective **15% final tax without imputation** (LN 188 of 2025) from **YA2025 (basis 2024)**; election by prescribed form; minimum 5-year lock-in; liability floored at the standard-imputation-system tax; not a QDMTT.

### YA2026 (MTCA, 7 May 2026)
- Page 2: TP + FITWI questions. Part 8 Q25/Part 9 Q4&Q6: Audit Exemption Rules [S.L.372.33]. Page 5: bank details field removed.
- **New TRA 135** (transfer pricing disclosure) and **TRA 136** (Art 14(1)(ma) permits/concessions/leases deduction).

### Pillar Two status (context)
- Malta applies the **Article 50(1) derogation** (Directive (EU) 2022/2523): **IIR and UTPR deferred** (permitted to 31 Dec 2029); **no QDMTT**. GloBE administrative provisions: S.L.123.212. Maltese constituent entities exempt from filing the TTIR locally; Maltese UPEs nominate a designated filing entity abroad. **LN 48 of 2026** removed the TTIR-filer notification obligation. Calendar-year groups in scope FY2024: first TTIR (abroad) due **30 June 2026**.

### Caveats
- YA2025 extension table reconstructed from Fenech & Fenech + Bloomberg Tax reports of the 25 Mar 2025 CfTC notice (official page not machine-readable).
