# Malta Company Income Tax Return — Tax Computation Reference (YA 2025/2026)

Corporate rate: **35%** flat on chargeable income; company return is a self-assessment, prepared from **audited financial statements**, filed by a **registered tax practitioner** (e-filing), due by **the later of 9 months after financial year-end or 31 March following the year of assessment** ([PwC Tax Administration](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/malta/corporate/tax-administration); [CSB Group – Corporate Tax Returns](https://www.csbgroup.com/malta-corporate-taxation/tax-return/); [MTCA corporate income tax](https://mtca.gov.mt/business-tax/corporate/Income_Tax)).

---

## 1. Capital allowances (wear and tear)

**Legal basis:** Deduction for Wear and Tear of Plant and Machinery Rules, S.L. 123.01 ([legislation.mt](https://legislation.mt/eli/sl/123.1/eng)); ITA Cap. 123 art. 14(1)(f)/(j) and art. 24 (balancing).

**Method:** straight-line only. Rates are expressed as a *minimum number of years* (i.e. maximum annual rate) ([PwC Deductions](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/malta/corporate/deductions)).

| Asset category | Min. years | Max. annual rate |
|---|---|---|
| Computers and electronic equipment | 4 | 25% |
| Computer software | 4 | 25% |
| Motor vehicles | 5 | 20% |
| Furniture, fixtures, fittings & soft furnishings | 10 | 10% |
| Equipment used for construction of buildings & excavation | 6 | 16.67% |
| Catering equipment | 6 | 16.67% |
| Aircraft airframe / engines / engine or airframe overhaul | 6 | 16.7% |
| Aircraft interiors and other parts | 4 | 25% |
| Ships and vessels | 10 | 10% |
| Electrical & plumbing installations and sanitary fittings | 15 | 6.67% |
| Cable infrastructure | 20 | 5% |
| Pipeline infrastructure | 20 | 5% |
| Communication and broadcasting equipment | 6 | 16.67% |
| Medical equipment | 6 | 16.67% |
| Lifts and escalators | 10 | 10% |
| Air conditioners | 6 | 16.67% |
| Equipment for production of water/electricity | 6 | 16.67% |
| Other machinery | 5 | 20% |
| Other plant | 10 | 10% |

Sources: [PwC Deductions](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/malta/corporate/deductions); aircraft rates: [Corrieri Cilia – Taxation of Aircraft Enterprises](https://corriericilia.com/publications/taxation-of-aircraft-enterprises-in-malta), [Chetcuti Cauchi – Aircraft Registration Tax](https://www.ccmalta.com/publications/malta-aircraft-registration-tax).

**Industrial buildings allowance (IBA):**
- **Initial allowance: 10%** of capital expenditure, in the year the building is first used.
- **Annual wear and tear: max 2%** of cost, **land cost excluded**.
- "Industrial building or structure" includes **hotels**, **car parks** (public commercial car parks first used after **1 Jan 2012**), and **office business centres > 2,500 sqm** first used after **1 Jan 2016** (only the centre owner claims).
- Total allowances over life ≤ 100% of cost; unclaimed amounts carry forward.
- Sources: [ACT – Capital Allowances on Industrial Buildings](https://www.act.com.mt/articles-publications/capital-allowances-on-industrial-buildings-or-structures/); [PwC Deductions](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/malta/corporate/deductions).

**Balancing statements (ITA art. 24):**
- Required when an asset on which allowances were claimed is **disposed of, destroyed, or put out of use** before cessation of the source.
- Proceeds > TWDV → **balancing charge** (taxable, capped at allowances previously granted); proceeds < TWDV → **balancing allowance**. Option to **reduce the cost of a replacement asset** instead of taking the balancing charge.
- **Transfers between related companies:** transferee's cost base = **lower of actual cost or TWDV** (adjusted for transferor balancing) — no step-up ([MIT COTC lecture](https://maintax.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/01/COTC-2022-2023-Module-B-Lecture-8-Deductions-and-Exemptions-Benjamin-Griscti-26012023.pdf); [PwC Deductions](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/malta/corporate/deductions)).

**Restrictions:**
- **Motor cars (non-commercial): cost base capped at €14,000**; lease payment deductions restricted proportionately to the same cap.
- Allowances only where **proper records/documentation** support cost; **proportional deduction** for part-business use.
- **Unabsorbed capital allowances: carried forward indefinitely but only against the SAME underlying source**; **lost if the source ceases**.

**Group transfer of capital allowances:**
- Normal group relief (arts 16–22 ITA) **excludes capital allowances** — only tax losses may be surrendered ([ACT – Group Relief Provisions](https://www.act.com.mt/news/allowable-deductions-surrendered-under-the-group-relief-provisions/)).
- Exception: **Group Deductions (Income Tax) Rules 2022 (LN 205/2022)** — one-off surrender of unabsorbed W&T + initial allowances for YA 2022 (COVID-19 Fiscal Assistance condition; group = 50%+1; cap €1,000,000 per group; FIA-attributable only against FIA income) ([BDO Malta](https://www.bdo.com.mt/en-gb/news/2022/intra-group-transfer-of-allowable-deductions); [Deloitte alert](https://www2.deloitte.com/mt/en/pages/tax/articles/alerts/2022/mt-tax-alert-22-13-Group-Deductions-Income-Tax-Rules-2022.html)).

## 2. Trading losses, capital losses, group relief

**Trading losses (ITA art. 14(1)(g)):**
- Carried forward **indefinitely**; **no carry-back**; set-off against total income (including capital gains) of subsequent years.
- Contrast: unabsorbed capital allowances are same-source only and lost on cessation.

**Capital losses:** offset **only against capital gains**; carried forward indefinitely against future capital gains.

**Group relief (ITA arts 16–22):**
- Group = 51% subsidiary relationship (>50% votes AND >50% distributable profits AND >50% winding-up assets), both companies Malta-resident and not resident elsewhere.
- Conditions: **coterminous accounting periods** and group membership **throughout the entire basis year**.
- Only **current-year losses** surrenderable — NOT brought-forward losses, NOT capital allowances (subject to LN 205/2022 exception).
- FIA-attributable losses only against claimant FIA income (art. 18).
- **Fiscal unity (Consolidated Group (Income Tax) Rules, S.L. 123.189):** parent holds **≥95%** of any two of votes/profits/winding-up assets; identical accounting periods; parent = principal taxpayer; intra-unit transactions disregarded ([PwC Group Taxation](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/malta/corporate/group-taxation)).

**Loss ordering with tax accounts:** allocation sequence **FTA → IPA → FIA → MTA → UA**; losses attach to the account of the source ([Papilio – Malta Tax Accounts](https://papilioservices.com/mt/tax-services/malta-tax-system/malta-tax-accounts/)).

## 3. Add-backs / non-deductible items

Base rule: **wholly and exclusively incurred in the production of the income** (ITA arts 14, 26).

Standard add-backs:
- **Accounting depreciation/amortisation** (replaced by capital allowances).
- **General provisions**; only specific proven bad debts deductible.
- **Fines and penalties**.
- **Charitable donations** (absent specific provision).
- **Entertainment/mixed private expenditure** (private element).
- **Motor vehicle leases** — restricted proportionately to the €14,000 cap.
- **Pre-trading expenditure** — only prescribed categories (**staff training, salaries/wages, advertising**) within **18 months** before commencement.

**ATAD Interest Limitation Rule (from basis years starting 1 Jan 2019):**
- Exceeding (net) borrowing costs deductible up to **30% of tax-adjusted EBITDA**.
- **De minimis: €3,000,000** of exceeding borrowing costs always deductible (test against NET exceeding costs, not gross).
- Disallowed costs: **carried forward indefinitely**; unused capacity: **carried forward 5 years**.
- Grandfathering: loans concluded **before 17 June 2016**; standalone/financial-undertaking carve-outs; long-term public infrastructure exclusion.
- ([ACT – ILR](https://www.act.com.mt/news/interest-limitation-rules-in-malta-under-atad-1/); [KPMG ATAD PDF](https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmg/mt/pdf/2024/04/maltese-implementation-of-the-anti-tax-avoidance-directive.pdf)).

**ATAD CFC rule (from 1 Jan 2019):** CFC = >50% held + foreign tax < 50% of Malta-equivalent charge; carve-outs (profits ≤€750k and non-trading income ≤€75k, or profits ≤10% of operating costs); attribution limited to non-genuine arrangements with Malta SPFs.

**ATAD exit tax (from 1 Jan 2020):** market-value exit charge on migrations/PE transfers; 5-year instalments for EU/EEA.

**Transfer pricing (basis years from 1 Jan 2024):** documentation/arm's-length requirements for cross-border related-party dealings (large companies).

## 4. Final-tax income excluded from the main computation (→ Final Tax Account)

- **15% final WHT on investment income** (arts 32–42): e.g. local bank interest. Final — not declared in the return; profits → FTA; no refunds on distribution. Election to receive gross possible → 35% in return.
- **15% final tax on rental income** (optional, gross, via TA24) — FTA.
- **Property transfer tax (art. 5A)** — final on transfer value: **8%** default; **10%** pre-2004 acquisitions; **5%** non-project within 5 years / restored UCA; **2%** sole residence within 3 years. Profits → FTA; no refunds.
- **Part-time 10%** (individuals; TA22).
- Refunds only arise on distributions from **MTA/FIA**; FTA/IPA/UA give **no refund**.

## 5. Common errors in Malta company tax returns

1. **Wrong tax account allocation** — ignoring FTA→IPA→FIA→MTA→UA sequence; property profits to MTA instead of IPA; final-taxed profits in chargeable income.
2. **Refund fraction errors** — 6/7 (trading), 5/7 (passive interest/royalties), 2/3 (double-tax relief claimed), 100% (participating holding); assuming 5% effective rate unconditionally.
3. **Missed/lapsed DDT10 determination** — valid **3 years**, must be renewed; loss of provisional-tax exemption and 18-month settlement deferral.
4. **Shareholders not registered for refunds** before distribution/claim (MTCA International & Corporate Tax Unit).
5. **Dividend warrant errors / missing documentation** — refund claim needs return + audited FS, dividend warrant (gross dividend, tax account, imputed tax), evidence of foreign tax, auditor's certificate where FRFTC claimed.
6. **Late/blocked refund claims** — refund processed only once tax paid and return + FS filed; practical timelines 8–14 months.
7. **NID miscalculation** — reference rate = **20-year MGS yield + 5%**; cap **90% of chargeable income**; excess carried forward; deemed interest income in shareholders' hands.
8. **Group relief with non-coterminous year ends** or partial-year membership; surrendering brought-forward losses/capital allowances; FIA restriction breach.
9. **Unabsorbed capital allowances set against other sources** — same-source only; lost on cessation.
10. **Ignoring the €14,000 car cap** (allowances and leases).
11. **Missing balancing statements** on disposal/scrapping; intra-group step-up instead of lower-of-cost/TWDV.
12. **Functional/reporting currency errors** — tax charged and refunds paid in the currency of share capital.
13. **Deadline/penalty exposure** — late filing €50→€1,500; interest **0.6% per month** on unpaid tax (0.33% before 31 Aug 2022), capped at the tax; e-filing extensions apply to the return, **NOT payment**.
14. **ILR misapplication** — €3M de minimis tested against net exceeding costs; 5-year capacity limit.
15. **Missing RA attachments** for tax credits (RA10 ME-approved credits, RA15 Micro Invest) — no schedule, no credit.
16. **Ignoring e-return verification checks** flagged by MTCA online service.
17. **Provisional tax mismatches** — PT benchmark not reconciled; assumed PT exemption without valid DDT10.
18. **Transfer pricing** documentation missing (from 2024).

## 6. Special regimes affecting return preparation

- **Tonnage tax / shipping:** licensed shipping organisations exempt on shipping activity income (Merchant Shipping Taxation Regs, amended 2018); ≥60% EU/EEA-flagged fleet condition; separate accounts mandatory; qualifying organisations may file a **declaration in lieu of a tax return**; dividends from exempt shipping profits exempt.
- **Aviation:** international transport aircraft income deemed to arise outside Malta (remittance basis for non-domiciled; FIA); accelerated W&T (airframe/engines 6 years, interiors 4).
- **Patent box (2019 Rules):** deduction = **95% × qualifying IP income × nexus ratio**; trademarks excluded; claimed in the return.
- **R&D (2024) Regulations:** credits/grants up to 80% of eligible costs, to 31 Dec 2026.
- **Malta Enterprise:** Invest 2024 (10–35% aid intensity, to 31 Dec 2026); **Micro Invest** 45% credit (65% Gozo), caps €50,000/€70,000; claimed via **RA10/RA15** with the return.
- **Pillar Two / elective 15% (FITWI):** Malta **deferred IIR/UTPR and opted against QDMTT until 2029** (Art. 50 derogation). **Final Income Tax Without Imputation Regulations** (published 2 Sep 2025): election from **YA 2025** for a flat, final **15%** on chargeable income — no imputation refunds/credits, **irrevocable for 5 years**; relevant for in-scope MNE constituent entities ([KPMG](https://kpmg.com/us/en/taxnewsflash/news/2025/09/tnf-malta-regulations-on-15-percent-elective-tax-published.html); [EY](https://www.ey.com/en_mt/newsroom/ey-newsletters/tax/tax-alert-03-09-25-)).
