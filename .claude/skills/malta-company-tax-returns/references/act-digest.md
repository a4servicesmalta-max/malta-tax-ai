## 1. KEY DEFINITIONS — Article 2(1)

### "Company" — Art. 2(1)
- (a)(i) limited liability company under the Companies Act (Cap. 386) or Commercial Partnerships Ordinance (Cap. 168); (ii) any other company constituted under Maltese law; (iii) a partnership *en nom collectif* or *en commandite* (Companies Act/CPO), a Civil Code Second Schedule registered partnership, an EEIG (S.L. 386.08), or (from year of assessment 2026, per Act III of 2026 art. 13(2)) a Special Limited Partnership Fund (S.L. 370.53) — in each case ONLY where it has elected to be treated as a company under Art. 27(6) Income Tax Management Act (ITMA, Cap. 372) and for as long as the election remains in force.
- Cell companies: every cell of a cell company, and the non-cellular part, is each deemed a SEPARATE company for all Income Tax Acts purposes.
- A partnership *en commandite* with capital divided into shares constituted before 1 January 2015 is DEEMED to have elected to be treated as a company (Art. 27(6) ITMA) for as long as the election remains in force.
- (b)(i) any foreign body of persons of a nature similar to (a)(i)/(ii); (ii) foreign body similar to the (a)(iii) partnerships, if it has elected under Art. 27(6) ITMA.
- (c) any co-operative society duly registered in Malta.

### "A company registered in Malta" — Art. 2(1)
A company resident in Malta; OR a company not resident in Malta which carries on any activity in Malta; if neither incorporated nor resident in Malta, it must be registered for this purpose with the Commissioner as prescribed.

### "Resident in Malta" — Art. 2(1)
- Individual: resides in Malta except for such temporary absences as seem reasonable to the Commissioner and not inconsistent with the claim of residence.
- Body of persons: any body of persons the CONTROL AND MANAGEMENT of whose business are exercised in Malta; PROVIDED that a company incorporated in Malta on or after 1 July 1994 SHALL BE resident in Malta (incorporation test — always resident), and any other Malta-incorporated company is resident from 1 January 1995 even where management and control are exercised outside Malta.

### "Body of persons" — Art. 2(1)
Any body corporate, including a company, and any fellowship, society or other association of persons, corporate or unincorporate, with or without legal personality.

### "Dividend" — Art. 2(1)
Includes (a) bonus shares; (b) any distribution by a company to partners/shareholders and any amount credited to them as such; (c) distributions/credits by a co-operative society to members (including patronage refund, bonus certificate, bonus share).

### "Bonus shares" — Art. 2(1)
Paid-up value of shares distributed by a company to shareholders to the extent the paid-up value represents a capitalization of profits.

### "Chargeable income" / "total income" — Art. 2(1)
- "Chargeable income" = total income of any person for any year.
- "Total income" = aggregate income from Part II sources, remaining AFTER Part III exemptions and Part IV deductions, computed under Part V. Proviso: subject to Art. 12(3)(b) ITMA, income not required to be disclosed and not disclosed does not form part of total income.
- "Income" includes capital gains as defined in Art. 5, EXCEPT for purposes of Art. 4(1) and Part IV (deductions).
- "Loss" (trade/business/profession/vocation) = loss computed in like manner as profits.

### "Equity holding" — Art. 2(1)
Holding of share capital in a company which is NOT a property company, where the shareholding entitles the shareholder to at least ANY TWO of: (i) right to votes; (ii) right to profits available for distribution; (iii) right to assets available for distribution on winding up ("equity holding rights"). Commissioner may determine an equity holding exists even where not a holding of share capital (or not solely), where in substance there is at any time entitlement to at least two equity holding rights.

### "Participating holding" — Art. 2(1) (feeds the Art. 12(1)(u) participation exemption)
Arises where:
- (a) a company holds DIRECTLY at least 5% of the equity shares of a company whose capital is wholly or partly divided into shares, conferring entitlement to at least 5% of any TWO of: (i) right to vote; (ii) profits available for distribution; (iii) assets available for distribution on winding up (Commissioner may treat satisfied via the substance proviso; and the 5% entitlement may exist "at any time" by reference to the equity-holding substance proviso); OR
- (b) equity shareholder with an OPTION to call for and acquire the entire balance of equity shares (to the extent permitted by the law of the country where the shares are held); OR
- (c) equity shareholder entitled to FIRST REFUSAL on proposed disposal, redemption or cancellation of all the other equity shares; OR
- (d) equity shareholder entitled to sit on the Board or appoint a director; OR
- (e) equity shareholder holding an investment of a minimum total value (at acquisition date(s)) of €1,164,000 (or foreign-currency equivalent), held for an uninterrupted period of not less than 183 days; OR
- (f) equity shareholder where the holding is for the furtherance of its own business and NOT held as trading stock for the purpose of a trade.
- Extension proviso: a holding in (a) a Maltese partnership/EEIG (Art. 2(1)(a)(iii) type) not being a property partnership and not having elected company treatment; (b) a similar foreign body (Art. 2(1)(b)(ii)) not being a property partnership, not having elected; or (c) a non-resident foreign collective investment vehicle where investors' liability is limited to the amount invested — is deemed a participating holding if it satisfies any of (a)–(f) mutatis mutandis ("shares" = capital entitling holder to at least two of vote/profits/winding-up assets).
- NOTE: because "equity holding" excludes property companies, a holding in a property company cannot be a participating holding.

### "Property company" — Art. 2(1)
A company which owns immovable property situated in Malta or any real rights thereon, or which holds directly/indirectly shares or other interests in any entity/person owning such property where 5% OR MORE of the total value of those shares/interests is attributable to such immovable property/rights. Carve-out: a company/entity/person carrying on a trade or business whose Malta immovable property consists ONLY of a factory, showroom, warehouse or office used solely for that trade/business is treated as not owning immovable property IF not more than 50% of the value of its assets consists of Malta immovable property/rights AND it does not carry on any activity whose income is derived directly or indirectly from Malta immovable property. ("Property partnership" — same test applied to partnerships.)

### "Passive interest or royalties" — Art. 2(1)
Interest or royalty income NOT derived directly or indirectly from a trade or business, where the interest/royalties have not suffered foreign tax, or suffered foreign tax (directly, by withholding, or otherwise) at a rate LESS THAN 5%.

### "Portfolio investment" — Art. 2(1)
Investment in securities (shares, bonds, etc.) held as one of many for risk-spreading, not strategic, made with no interest in and without intention of influencing management, made only to follow share price/dividend policy to maximise returns and to sell as soon as shares may lose value.

### "Distributable profits" and the FIVE TAX ACCOUNTS — Art. 2(1)
- "Distributable profits" = total profits available for distribution by a company registered in Malta under Maltese law, allocated to: **final tax account (FTA), immovable property account (IPA), foreign income account (FIA), Maltese taxed account (MTA), and untaxed account (UA)**. Proviso: for a company neither incorporated nor resident in Malta, distributable profits = profits attributable to its Malta activities registered with the Commissioner, less profits distributed in previous years.
- "Taxed account(s)" = any or all of FTA, IPA, FIA, MTA and UA.
- **Final tax account (FTA)**: the taxed account to which an amount of distributable profits which suffered tax, calculated in such manner/amount as may be prescribed, is allocated BEFORE any distributable profits are allocated to any other taxed account (first in allocation order).
- **Immovable property account (IPA)**: the taxed account to which distributable profits which have suffered tax and which are NOT allocated to the FTA, calculated as prescribed, are allocated BEFORE allocation to the other taxed accounts (second in order).
- **Foreign income account (FIA)** (with effect from the year preceding year of assessment 1996): the following categories of distributable profits to the extent they result from TAXABLE income:
  - (a) profits from royalties and similar income arising OUTSIDE Malta; from dividends, capital gains, interest, rents, income or gains derived from a participating holding or from its disposal — OTHER THAN a participating holding in a Malta-resident company or Malta-resident partnership en commandite with capital not divided into shares; and any other income from investments situated outside Malta — which are liable to tax in Malta and shown as part of chargeable income in the Art. 10 ITMA return, receivable by a company registered in Malta not being an MFSA Act (Cap. 330)-registered company;
  - (b) profits from investments, assets or liabilities situated outside Malta of a Malta-licensed bank or Financial Institutions Act (Cap. 376) licensee (not MFSA Act-registered); proviso: from year of assessment 2016 not applicable to a company not specifically empowered to receive such profits/gains;
  - (c) all profits or gains of a company registered in Malta, liable to tax in Malta and shown in the return, attributable to a PERMANENT ESTABLISHMENT (including a branch) situated outside Malta — computed as if the PE were an independent enterprise operating in similar conditions at arm's length;
  - (d) profits resulting from dividends paid out of the FIA of another company registered in Malta;
  - (e) profits of a Malta insurance company (Art. 7 Insurance Business Act) from insurance of risks situated outside Malta — only for companies that allocated to FIA under this paragraph up to and including year of assessment 2007, and only up to and including year of assessment 2011 (and only for profits arising in the year preceding year of assessment 2000 and later).
  - Banking proviso: a Malta-licensed bank / member of a "banking group" may only allocate to FIA if MORE THAN 95% of its average daily deposits throughout the financial year are taken from persons not resident in Malta (group test on consolidated basis). "Banking group": Maltese registered companies, at least one a Malta-licensed bank, members if one is a 10% affiliate of the other or both of a third company (10% affiliate = >10% of ordinary share capital AND >10% of voting rights owned directly/indirectly; or beneficial entitlement >10% of distributable profits; or >10% of winding-up assets). A company acquired by a bank in satisfaction of a debt, not otherwise part of the bank's business, is deemed not part of the banking group.
  - International trading company profits: NOT allocated to FIA.
  - Transitional: profits derived up to 31 December 2010 by a company resident in Malta before 1 January 2007 (other than one which exercised the Art. 48(4A)(b) ITMA proviso (i)(2) option) which would have been FIA profits had they been charged in year of assessment 2007, are allocated to the FIA.
- **Maltese taxed account (MTA)**: profits not included in the FIA which (a) have suffered tax; or (b) were exempt under Maltese law where the distribution is also exempt in the shareholders' hands — paragraph (b) ceased to apply with effect from year of assessment 2008.
- **Untaxed account (UA)**: profits (or losses) representing total distributable profits (positive) or total accumulated losses (negative), MINUS the total amounts allocated to the other taxed accounts (residual; may be negative).

### "International trading company" — Art. 2(1) (historic)
Company registered in Malta by not later than 31 December 2006, engaged solely in trading with non-resident persons outside Malta, objects expressly limited accordingly. **No company is an international trading company with effect from 1 January 2011.**

### Other definitions relevant to company returns — Art. 2(1)
- "Certified public auditor": warrant holder under the Accountancy Profession Act (or registered partnership of auditors), not disqualified under Art. 153 Companies Act.
- "Collective investment scheme": scheme licensed under the Investment Services Act (Cap. 370) or notified under the Notified AIFs Regulations.
- "Commissioner": Commissioner for Tax and Customs.
- "Debenture interest": interest payable by a company under a debenture or debenture trust deed (mortgage or other instrument acknowledging indebtedness).
- "Industrial building or structure": includes a building used as a hotel or a car park or offices, as may be prescribed. "Hotel" includes grouped constructions suitably furnished, with common ancillary hotel services within a single defined parcel, common management, accommodation of paying guests. "Car park": commercial structure available to the general public which is the main income-generating activity of a person claiming deductions for it under Art. 14(1)(f) or (j), or whose operation involves substantial activity (capital employed, organisation, income), first used for this purpose after 1 January 2012.
- "Malta": islands of the Maltese Archipelago including territorial waters, continental shelf (Cap. 535) and any exclusive economic zone area (Cap. 625).
- "Tax return date": date prescribed under Art. 10(1) ITMA for submission of the return for that year of assessment. "Tax settlement date": meaning per Art. 42(1A) ITMA. "Self assessment": per Art. 10(2)-(3) ITMA.
- "Year of assessment": 12-month period commencing 1 January 1949 and each subsequent 12-month period.
- "Prescribed": prescribed by rule under this Act. "The Income Tax Acts": this Act plus the ITMA (Cap. 372).
- Art. 2(2): words unknown to Maltese law but known to English law take their English-law meaning so far as necessary.

## 2. ADMINISTRATION — Article 3
Administration of the Act vested in the Commissioner; Arts. 3 and 4 ITMA apply to his powers and functions.

## 3. CHARGE TO TAX — Article 4

### Art. 4(1) — Charging provision
Income tax is payable at the rate(s) specified for each year of assessment upon: capital gains as defined in Art. 5 (from year of assessment 1993, only for gains made on or after 25 November 1992), accruing or derived from Malta or elsewhere, whether received in Malta or not; and (from year of assessment 1949) upon income accruing in or derived from Malta or elsewhere, whether received in Malta or not, in respect of:
- (a) gains or profits from any trade, business, profession or vocation (for whatever period carried on), including profit from sale of property acquired for profit-making by sale, or from any profit-making undertaking or scheme;
- (b) gains or profits from employment or office, including the value of benefits (fringe benefit regulations may be prescribed);
- (c) dividends, premiums, interest (including gains on money in any currency deposited with a Banking Act bank in any account) or discounts. Proviso: income of a company from an OFFSHORE BANKING SUBSIDIARY constitutes income chargeable under paragraph (a) and Art. 56(6) applies to it;
- (d) any pension, charge, annuity or annual payment;
- (e) rents, royalties, premiums and any other profits arising from property;
- (f) repealed (Act XX of 1996);
- (g) gains or profits not falling under the foregoing.

### Art. 4(1) provisos — jurisdictional basis (critical for non-dom companies)
- (i) income arising OUTSIDE Malta to a person NOT ordinarily resident in Malta OR NOT domiciled in Malta: tax payable on the amount RECEIVED in Malta (remittance basis).
- (ii) NO tax on capital gains arising outside Malta to a person not ordinarily resident or not domiciled in Malta — foreign capital gains of non-doms are not taxable even if remitted.
- (iv) EEZ activities: only activities licensed/authorised under the Exclusive Economic Zone Act deemed carried out in Malta; transfers of assets in the EEZ deemed to take place in Malta (Arts. 5 and 5A apply).

### Art. 4(2)–(7) — supplementary
- 4(2): sums realized under insurance against LOSS OF PROFITS are taken into account in ascertaining profits or income.
- 4(3): Malta agricultural/manufacturing/productive undertakings — (a) sale of product in a wholesale market outside Malta or for delivery outside Malta: FULL profits deemed Malta-source, unless increase in profit due to treatment outside Malta other than handling, grading, blending, sorting, packing or disposal; (b) other disposal/use of product outside Malta: deemed profit = best-advantage wholesale market price outside Malta.
- 4(4): clubs/similar institutions — if not less than one half of gross receipts on revenue account (including entrance fees and subscriptions) come from members, not deemed to carry on a business; if less than half, whole income from transactions with members and others deemed business receipts.
- 4(5): a BALANCING CHARGE under Art. 24 is deemed income chargeable to tax.
- 4(6)–(7): winding up of approved pension/saving/provident funds — chargeable in the fund's hands.

## 4. STEP-UP ON BECOMING MALTA-RESIDENT/DOMICILED — Article 4A
Where a person (i) becomes resident in Malta having never been domiciled or resident in Malta before; (ii) becomes domiciled in Malta having never been domiciled or resident before; or (iii) is "a company resulting from the merger" registered in Malta under reg. 3(2) Cross-border Mergers Regulations (S.L. 386.12) where none of its assets on merger day was owned by a merging company domiciled/resident in Malta before the merger — and elects by notice in writing to the Commissioner: all assets situated OUTSIDE Malta acquired before the occurrence are deemed acquired on the occurrence date at MARKET VALUE at that date. Election deadline: not later than the end of the year of assessment immediately following the basis year in which the occurrence takes place.

## 5. CAPITAL GAINS — Article 5

### Art. 5(1) — chargeable capital gains (charged under Art. 4(1))
- (a)(i) transfer of ownership/usufruct of immovable property or assignment/cession of rights over it;
- (a)(ii) transfer of ownership/usufruct of, or assignment/cession of rights over, SECURITIES, business, goodwill, business permits, copyright, patents, trademarks, trade-names and any other intellectual property;
- (a)(iii) transfer of beneficial interest in a trust;
- (a)(iv) transfers of securities under sub-art. (9A) (degrouping) and transfers of value in securities under sub-art. (13)(b)(ii);
- (a)(v) transfer of ownership/usufruct of or rights over any INTEREST IN A PARTNERSHIP (including deemed transfer where a person acquires/increases a partnership share).
- (b) Definitions: "securities" = shares, stocks and like instruments participating in any way in the profits of the company whose return is NOT limited to a fixed rate of return; units in a CIS; units relating to linked long-term insurance business. "Transfer" includes assignment, sale, emphyteusis/sub-emphyteusis, partition, donation, settlement on trust, distribution/reversion of trust property, sale by instalments, any alienation under any title including redemption/liquidation/cancellation of CIS units, maturity/surrender of linked long-term policies, deemed transfers under (9A)/(13)(b), and transfers of assets by a company to its shareholders in winding up or under a scheme of distribution; EXCLUDES transfers *causa mortis*.

### Art. 5(3) — computation for shares/securities
- Shares acquired after 25 Nov 1992: cost of acquisition. Shares acquired before: higher of equity/NAV method and price.
- **Where an amount standing to the credit of a company's reserve accounts (other than capital redemption reserve and profits available for distribution) is applied in paying up allotted shares, the cost of acquisition of such shares is ZERO.**
- Listed-share transfers (recognised exchange, not CIS, not exempt under 5(6)(b)): transfer value capped at market value immediately upon admission to listing.

### Art. 5(5)/(6)/(6A) — exclusions/exemptions
- 5(6): securities gains excluded for: (b) transfer of shares LISTED (or in consequence of a listing) on a recognised stock exchange, not being CIS securities; (c) listed CIS securities held in a prescribed fund; (d) linked long-term insurance units referencing (b)/(c) securities; (e) spousal/community transfers.

### Art. 5(7) — interaction with capital allowances
Where capital allowances under Art. 14(1)(f)/(j) were claimed and the asset is sold above cost plus improvements, cost of acquisition is computed on cost plus improvements.

### Art. 5(8) — ROLLOVER RELIEF (replacement of business assets)
Asset used in a business for at least 3 years, transferred and replaced within 1 year by an asset used SOLELY for a similar purpose in the business: gain not taxed; cost of new asset reduced by the gain. If the gain exceeds the cost of the replacement, the excess is taxable in the year the replacement was acquired and the replacement's cost on subsequent transfer is zero.

### Art. 5(9) — INTRA-GROUP TRANSFERS (no gain/no loss)
- (i) Transfer of an asset between companies which are (a) a group for Art. 16 purposes, or (b) controlled and beneficially owned directly/indirectly to the extent of MORE THAN 50% by the same shareholders: deemed no loss or gain. On a subsequent transfer outside the group, base cost and acquisition date = original cost/date before the intra-group transfer.
- (iii) Where the asset is Malta immovable property or shares in a property company: relief applies ONLY where the individual direct/indirect beneficial owners of both companies are the SAME and each holds substantially the same percentage of nominal share capital and voting rights in each. "Substantially the same" = difference in percentage interest ≤20%. Disapplied where the companies are ≥80% directly/indirectly owned by a company listed on a recognised stock exchange.
- (iv) Where the acquiring company issues shares in exchange for the asset: the cost of acquisition of those shares is reduced (not below zero) by (transfer value of asset − transferor's cost).

### Art. 5(9A) — DEGROUPING CHARGE (shares)
If a company holds shares acquired intra-group exempt under 5(9) and ceases to be a member of the ORIGINAL GROUP before the lapse of SIX YEARS from the acquisition: deemed transfer and re-acquisition; gain/loss accrues immediately before it leaves the group. Only applies where the target company owned (directly/indirectly, 5% attribution test) Malta immovable property at acquisition and still owns it (or part) at exit. JOINT ELECTION available to treat the gain/loss as accruing to a related Malta-incorporated company with provisional tax at 35% of market value of shares deemed transferred.

### Art. 5(10) — CAPITAL LOSSES
(a) computed in the same manner as a capital gain; (b) losses from Art. 5(1) transactions may NOT be set off against other income; carried forward and set off against CAPITAL GAINS of subsequent years until fully absorbed (ring-fenced); (c) bad debts relating to such transactions deductible against capital gains.

### Art. 5(11)–(13)
- 5(11): amounts charged under Art. 4(1)(a) (trading) are not charged again as capital gains for the same transaction.
- 5(13)(b)(i): a REDUCTION OF SHARE CAPITAL is deemed a transfer of a proportionate part of the holding EXCEPT proportionate reductions across all shareholders.
- 5(13)(b)(ii): VALUE SHIFTING — where the market value of a person's shares is reduced by a change in issued share capital or voting rights, and the value passes into shares/rights held by another person, the transferor is deemed to transfer that value. NOT applicable where: no change in beneficial owners and proportions; exempt 5(14) restructuring; listed company; close-family transfers; or non-property company + bona fide commercial reasons.

### Art. 5(14) — EXCHANGE OF SHARES ON RESTRUCTURING
Exchange of shares on restructuring of holdings upon mergers, demergers, divisions, amalgamations and reorganisation: deemed NO loss or gain; cost carryover. Applies only as prescribed by the Minister.

### Art. 5(15) — INCORPORATION OF A BUSINESS
Business or partnership *en nom collectif* incorporated as a going concern into a company beneficially owned as to NOT LESS THAN 75% by the same person(s): deemed no loss/gain; wholly in exchange for shares.

## 6. PROPERTY TRANSFERS TAX (FINAL WITHHOLDING ON MALTA IMMOVABLES) — Article 5A

### Scope — Art. 5A(1)–(3)
Tax on any transfer of immovable property situated in Malta or rights over it, on/after 1 November 2005, on TRANSFER VALUE (not gain). Assignments of rights under a promise of sale (konvenju) are separately regulated. Exclusions include: (f) judicial sale by auction/court winding up; (g) replacement-of-business-asset rollover (property used in business ≥3 years, replaced within 1 year; election by declaration to notary; disapplied if replacement disposed of within 2 years); (h) transfer by a NON-RESIDENT with home-tax-authority certificate (not owned/controlled by Malta residents); (j) project property of a company that issued LISTED public debt securities for the project (election → Art 27G).

### Exemptions — Art. 5A(4)
Includes: (f) INTRA-GROUP transfer qualifying under Art. 5(9); (g) incorporation of a going concern satisfying 5(15); (i) transfers by exempt persons; (j) DISTRIBUTION IN WINDING UP to an individual shareholder owning ≥95% for >5 years where the company held the property as a capital asset >5 years — **profits from an exempt 5A(4)(j) transfer go to the FINAL TAX ACCOUNT.**

### Rates — Art. 5A(5) (tax on TRANSFER VALUE)
- (a) Default: **8%** of transfer value (post-1 Jan 2015 transfers).
- (b) Acquired *causa mortis* after 24 Nov 1992 or by donation >5 years before: **12% of excess over acquisition value**.
- (c) *Causa mortis* before 25 Nov 1992 / judicial auction: **7%**.
- (d) Art. 31C(1) circumstances: **10%**.
- (e) Non-project property transferred within 5 years of acquisition: **5%**.
- (f) Acquired before 1 Jan 2004 (no pre-17 Nov 2014 promise-of-sale notice): **10%**.
- (g) Individual's sole ordinary residence within 3 years: **2%**.
- (h) UCA/scheduled restored property: **5%**.

### Mechanics — Art. 5A(6)–(13)
- (6): TRANSFER VALUE = HIGHER of market value and consideration.
- (9): NO deductions; no losses from 5A transfers deductible against anything.
- (10)(a): tax is FINAL — not creditable, not refundable, no provisional tax.
- **(10)(d): every Malta-resident company allocates distributable profits from 5A transfers to the FINAL TAX ACCOUNT.**
- (11): remitted within 15 WORKING DAYS of transfer via the notary.
- (12): Commissioner may order additional tax within 6 YEARS; undervaluation-only orders require declared value < 85% of market value and 12 months.
- (12A): PROPERTY DEGROUPING — company leaves group within 6 years of exempt intra-group property acquisition: 8% (or 10% for pre-2004 acquisitions) of the intra-group acquisition value; due within 15 working days of exit; joint election to shift to related company available.
- (12B): where capital allowances (14(1)(f)/(j)) were claimed on the property and an Art. 24(1) event occurs: BALANCING STATEMENT must be filed with the return; balancing charge taxed at Art. 56 rates (35% company) notwithstanding 5A finality.

## 7. EXPATRIATE CONCESSIONS — Article 6
Investment services / insurance expatriates: 10-year option excluding specified employer-provided benefits from tax; treated as NOT resident for Art. 12(1)(c) purposes.

## 8. HISTORIC PROVISIONS — Articles 7–9A
YA 1973 tax holiday; 1972 amnesty; 1995 spontaneous declaration scheme. Obsolete for current returns.

## RETURN-PREPARER CHECKPOINTS FROM THIS PART
1. **Residence**: Malta-incorporated company (on/after 1 Jul 1994) is ALWAYS resident; foreign-incorporated company is resident if managed and controlled in Malta.
2. **Basis of taxation** (Art. 4(1) provisos): resident AND domiciled → worldwide income and gains; resident-but-not-domiciled (e.g., foreign-incorporated, Malta-managed) → Malta-source income/gains + foreign income only if received in Malta; foreign CAPITAL gains never taxable for such companies even if remitted.
3. **Tax accounts**: FTA allocated first, then IPA, then FIA/MTA; UA is the residual. Art. 5A profits → FTA (Art. 5A(10)(d)); exempt 5A(4)(j) winding-up distribution profits → FTA. FIA content per the Art. 2(1) definition.
4. **Participation-exemption gatekeeping**: verify "equity holding" (2 of 3 rights, not a property company) then "participating holding" via any of tests (a)–(f) (≥5% / option / first refusal / director / €1,164,000 + 183 days / business furtherance not trading stock).
5. **Capital gains schedule**: ring-fenced capital losses carried forward (Art. 5(10)); intra-group no-gain/no-loss with >50% common ownership (Art. 5(9)); ±20% same-individual-owners condition for Malta immovables/property-company shares; 6-year degrouping clawbacks (Arts. 5(9A) and 5A(12A)); rollover relief (Art. 5(8)); listed-share exemption (Art. 5(6)(b)); share-for-share restructuring relief (Art. 5(14)); business incorporation relief 75% (Art. 5(15)); zero cost for shares paid up out of non-distributable reserves (Art. 5(3)).
6. **Property transfers**: Art. 5A tax is final on transfer VALUE (8% default; 12%/10%/7%/5%/2% variants), no deductions, no credit/refund, payable via notary within 15 working days; balancing statement obligation where capital allowances were claimed (Art. 5A(12B)).
7. **Balancing charges** are chargeable income (Art. 4(5)); loss-of-profits insurance proceeds are income (Art. 4(2)).

# Malta Income Tax Act (CAP. 123) — Digest of Part 2 (pages 74–146; Articles 9B–41A)

**Scope note:** This part covers Articles 9B through 41A(g). Basis: tax is charged for a year of assessment (YA) on income of the year immediately preceding it (Art 10).

---

## 1. Basis of Assessment and Accounting Periods

- **Art 10 — Basis of assessment.** Tax is charged, levied and collected for each year of assessment upon the chargeable income of the year immediately preceding the year of assessment.
- **Art 11 — Accounting periods.** (1) Every person must make up accounts to 31 December. (2) The Commissioner may permit an accounting date other than 31 December; gains/profits are then computed on the income of the year terminating on that permitted date in the year preceding the YA. (3) Available only to: (a) a company; (b) a non-electing commercial partnership; (c) a body corporate established by law; (d) an Art 30(7)(d) undertaking. (4) Commissioner may impose binding conditions on the change.

## 2. Exemptions Relevant to Companies (Art 12)

### 2.1 Non-resident investors (Art 12(1)(c))
- **(c)(i)** Interest, discount, premium or royalties accruing to/derived by a person **not resident in Malta** are exempt. Exception: not where the non-resident carries on trade/business in Malta through a PE with which the royalties/debt claim are effectively connected.
- **(c)(ii)** Gains/profits derived by a non-resident on a transfer of: units in a CIS, linked long-term insurance units, an interest in a partnership which is **not a property partnership**, and shares or securities in a company which is **not a property company** — exempt.
- **Common proviso:** the beneficial owner must be a non-resident and must not be owned and controlled by, directly or indirectly, nor act on behalf of, individual(s) ordinarily resident AND domiciled in Malta.

### 2.2 Dividend exemption for individuals (Art 12(1)(c)(iii))
- Dividends paid by a Malta-registered company to an **individual** out of profits allocated to any of the **taxed accounts other than the final tax account and the untaxed account** are exempt where the individual's chargeable income excluding the dividend equals or exceeds the "relevant threshold" (€28,700 married rates; €19,500 / €21,200 others — YA 2027 amendments). Listed-share carve-out: <0.5% holders of listed shares.

### 2.3 Participation exemption (Art 12(1)(u)(1))
- Any income or gains derived by a **company registered in Malta** from a **participating holding** or from the **transfer** of such holding is exempt, **where the taxpayer has not shown such income/gain as part of chargeable income in the return** (exemption applied by non-inclusion; showing it in the return forgoes it — that is the "election to tax").
- **Anti-abuse conditions for DIVIDENDS** from a participating holding — exemption applies only if EITHER:
  - (i) the body of persons satisfies ANY ONE of: (1) resident or incorporated in an EU country/territory; (2) subject to any foreign tax of at least **15%**; (3) not more than **50%** of its income derived from **passive interest or royalties**; OR
  - (ii) if none satisfied, BOTH: (1) the holding is **not a portfolio investment** (a holding in a non-resident body deriving >50% of income from portfolio investments is deemed a portfolio investment); AND (2) the body or its passive interest/royalties have been subject to foreign tax at **not less than 5%**.
- GAINS on transfer of a participating holding: no anti-abuse conditions (but see Malta-resident-holdings proviso).
- **Malta-resident holdings proviso:** gains on transfer of a participating holding in a Malta-resident entity qualify only if the gains would have been exempt under Art 12(1)(c)(ii) had the transfer been made by the **beneficial owner** of the transferor company; pro rata for multiple beneficial owners.
- **Clawback:** where exemption claimed and a "non-qualifying shareholder" later becomes beneficially entitled to the gains, the untaxed gains are taxed at the **Art 56(6) rate** in the YA of entitlement.
- **EU non-cooperative jurisdiction exclusion:** no exemption for income from a participating holding in a body resident in a jurisdiction on the **EU non-cooperative list for ≥3 months** during the basis year, unless sufficient significant people functions are proven there.

### 2.4 Foreign PE exemption (Art 12(1)(u)(2))
- Income or gains of a Malta-registered company **attributable to a PE (including a branch) situated outside Malta**, or to the transfer of such PE, are exempt **where not shown as chargeable income in the return**. Computed as if the PE were an independent arm's-length enterprise.
- **Anti-avoidance:** series of transactions with sole/main purpose of reducing tax via this provision → assessable as if it did not apply.
- **Anti-hybrid (Parent-Subsidiary Directive):** from 1 Jan 2016, participation exemption applies to EU-sourced distributed profits **only to the extent not deductible by the subsidiary** in its Member State.

### 2.5 Other exemptions affecting companies
- **12(1)(d):** licensed retirement funds/schemes (other than Malta immovable property income).
- **12(1)(k):** profits of a non-resident shipowner, conditional on **reciprocity**.
- **12(1)(q):** income of a co-operative society.
- **12(1)(s):** income of a **collective investment scheme** (other than Malta immovable property income and Art 41A(a) investment income).
- **12(1)(v):** royalties/advances from **patents, copyright, trademarks** exempt subject to prescribed conditions; dividends out of such exempt profits exempt in shareholders' hands with corporate flow-through. Not applicable if shown as chargeable income in the return.
- **Proviso to 12(1):** exemption does not remove the obligation to **file a return** for such income.

### 2.6 Investment Registration Scheme (Art 9B)
- **9B(5) — loss forfeiture:** a person who availed of the 2014 IRS forfeits carry-forward of **losses under Art 5(10)(a) and Art 14(1)(g)** declared for YA 2013 and earlier.

## 3. Deductions (Art 14) — Core Rules

**General rule (Art 14(1)):** deduct all outgoings and expenses **to the extent wholly and exclusively incurred in the production of the income**, including:

- **(a) Borrowing costs** — wholly and exclusively for the trade/business or on capital employed in acquiring income; subject to limitations per Art 96(2) guidelines (interest limitation / ATAD ILR).
- **(b) Rent** paid by a tenant of land/buildings occupied for acquiring the income.
- **(c) Repairs** of premises, plant or machinery; renewal/repair/alteration of implements.
- **(d) Bad debts** in a trade/business, proved to have become bad during the basis year. Recoveries = trading receipts of the year of recovery.
- **(e) Employer contributions** to approved pension/saving/provident funds.
- **(f) Wear and tear (capital allowances)** on plant and machinery, and industrial buildings/structures, used in producing income. Also available to a user bearing the burden of wear and tear on property not belonging to him. Provisos: (i) industrial building max **2% of cost per year, excluding land**; (ii) unabsorbed allowances under (f)/(j) **carried forward against the same source** indefinitely; (iii) aggregate (f)+(j) deductions cannot exceed **original cost**.
- **(g) Trading losses:**
  - Loss incurred in any trade/business in the basis year. Computed with all deductions **except (f) and (j)** (capital allowances carried forward separately, not part of the trading loss).
  - Set off first against **capital gains or income from other sources of the same year**; excess **carried forward against total income of subsequent years** (indefinite).
  - No set-off of a foreign loss which, had it been a profit retained outside Malta, would not have been chargeable (remittance-basis symmetry).
  - A loss shall NOT be deducted against income allocable to the **FTA**; a loss from FTA-type activities is **not an allowable loss** (disappears).
  - Subject to group relief, no person may deduct another person's loss.
  - **Mergers/divisions** (S.L. 123.140): Commissioner may permit losses/allowances to transfer to the successor company.
- **(h) Scientific research**: capital-nature expenditure spread 1/6 per year over 6 years.
- **(ha) R&D&I super-deduction [from YA 2027, Act III of 2026]:** 175% of actual expenditure.
- **(j) Initial allowances:** plant and machinery first used in basis year — **20%** initial; industrial building first used — **10%** initial.
- **(l) Promotional expenditure:** market research, advertising, samples, fairs.
- **(m) Capital expenditure on IP:** spread equally over **not less than 3 consecutive years**; [from YA 2024] may be deducted **in full in year 1** subject to prescribed conditions. Intra-group (5(9)) transferred IP: acquirer's deduction capped at lower of cost and market value less transferor's claims.
- **(ma) Business permits/concessions/commercial leases (capex on/after 1 Jan 2025):** spread over 15 years or shorter acquired period; no deduction for indefinite/15+ year durations; written document required; no deduction on related-party acquisitions (>25% tests).
- **(n)** Childcare payments — max **€935 per child**.
- **(o) Notional interest deduction** (risk capital) — as prescribed (NID Rules S.L. 123.176).
- **(p) Patent box** deduction as prescribed.

**Art 14(3) — Pre-trading expenditure:** incurred ≤**18 months** before commencement, of a kind deductible under 14(1), treated as incurred on day 1 of trading (as prescribed — staff training/salaries per rules).
**Art 14(4) — Related-party property services/finance cap** (anti-avoidance, >25% common ownership).
**Art 14(5) — VAT invoice condition:** no deduction unless in possession of the required tax invoice/document.

## 4. Special Deduction Regimes
- **Art 15/15A — Investment services companies and insurance managers** (licensed 1 Oct 2003): 100% extra deductions windows; **15(5): these additional deductions do NOT count in determining losses for group relief.**

## 5. Group Relief Provisions (Arts 16–22)

- **Art 16 — Group definition:** two companies **resident in Malta, neither resident elsewhere**, are a group if one is the **51% subsidiary** of the other or both of a third Malta-resident company. 51% subsidiary = (a) **>50% of ordinary share capital AND >50% voting rights** (direct/indirect); AND (b) beneficial entitlement to **>50% of distributable profits**; AND (c) **>50% of winding-up assets**.
- **Art 17 — Surrender:** current-year allowable losses may be surrendered on claim. Both companies must be group members **throughout the basis year**. A **payment for group relief** (up to the surrendered amount) is ignored for tax and is not a distribution.
- **Art 18 — Losses surrenderable:**
  - (1)(a) Requires **coterminous accounting periods** (begin and end on same dates). Currency conversion at CBM mean rate on last day of period where tax currencies differ.
  - (1)(b) Losses may be surrendered **in excess of the claimant's total income**; excess carried forward by claimant under 14(1)(g).
  - (1)(c)/(d) **Tax-account matching:** IPA/MTA-type losses only against claimant IPA/MTA income; FIA-type losses only against FIA income (rule 9 elections ignored).
  - (2) Newly incorporated company (same period-end) or company wound up mid-period (same start): deemed coterminous.
- **Art 19 — Anti-avoidance:** arrangements to make/unmake group membership for tax → treated as not a group member.
- **Art 20 — No double relief.**
- **Art 21 — Claims:** need not be full amount; require **consent of surrendering company**; deadline = **later of** the tax return date or **12 months after the end of the accounting period**.
- **Art 22 — "Allowable loss" = current-year 14(1)(g) loss only — NOT brought-forward losses.** "Total income" computed before group relief.
- **Art 22A — Fiscal unity enabling provision** (basis of Consolidated Group (Income Tax) Rules).
- **Art 22B — Elective tax [from YA 2025, Act IX of 2025]:** regulations may charge an elective higher tax on profits of "Malta entities" (Pillar Two-related elective mechanism).

## 6. Petroleum Contractors (Art 23)
- PSC contractor rules; each contract/field a separate source. **(4) Group relief does NOT apply.** (5) 10% withholding on non-resident sub-contractor payments for Malta services, remitted within 30 days, final unless notified provisional.

## 7. Balancing Statements, Allowances and Charges (Art 24)
- Trigger: property with (f)/(j) claims sold/transferred/destroyed/put out of use before source ceases.
- **Balancing statement rendered with the tax return** showing (i) capital expenditure, (ii) total depreciation allowed, (iii) proceeds.
- **Balancing allowance** = (i) − [(ii)+(iii)] if positive. **Balancing charge** = [(ii)+(iii)] − (i) if positive, **capped at total allowances previously granted**.
- **(3) Replacement election (rollover):** balancing charge deferred against replacement asset's cost.
- (8) Unabsorbed balancing allowances carry forward against the same source.

## 8. Ordering and Disallowed Deductions
- **Art 25 — Ordering rule:** expenses of income allocated to a taxed account must first be deducted against such income.
- **Art 26 — Deductions NOT allowed:**
  - (a) domestic/private expenses;
  - (b) expenses not wholly and exclusively incurred in producing the income;
  - (c) capital loss/withdrawal/expenditure (save Arts 14, 23);
  - (d) cost of **improvements**;
  - (e) losses/expenses **recoverable under insurance or indemnity**;
  - (f) rent of premises not used for the income;
  - (g) **voluntary payments**;
  - (h) **interest/discount/premium paid to a related non-resident** financing Malta immovable property where exempt in the recipient's hands under 12(1)(c)(i) (related = >10% common ownership/control);
  - (i) payments constituting a **criminal offence**.

## 9. Insurance Companies (Art 27)
- Prescriptive computation of total income for general and long-term business (technical provisions movements, premiums, recoveries, investment income). 27(2): 15% final tax on determinable amounts under long-term contracts to Malta residents, payable by 14th of following month.

## 10. Corporate Reorganisations and Special Vehicles
- **Art 27A:** Merger Directive-style rules enabling provision (S.L. 123.140).
- **Art 27D — Trusts treated as companies:** irrevocable election (30 days) for investment-income-only trusts; taxed at 35% with company-style tax accounts.
- **Art 27E/27F:** foundations; conversions.

## 11. Property Developers Election (Art 27G)
- For Art 5A(3)(j) electors (listed-bond project companies): project transfer income = **separate chargeable income at 35%**; provisional tax **8% of consideration** (10% pre-2004 acquisitions); unutilised provisional tax formula (0.625×A − B) not refundable; project losses ring-fenced (no group relief); **no refund to dividend recipients; profits → FTA**.

## 12. Shipping, Aviation, Telecoms (Arts 28–29)
- **Art 28:** non-resident shipowners — Malta shipping receipts deemed Malta-source; certificate-based profit-ratio computation.
- **Art 29(2) — Aviation:** income from **ownership, leasing or operation of aircraft or aircraft engines used in international transport** is **deemed to arise outside Malta** regardless of registration or Malta calls (→ remittance basis for non-domiciled companies; FIA-allocable).

## 13. Ecclesiastical Entities (Art 30) — 20% rate context.

## 14. Dividends — Gross-Up (Art 31)
- Income from a dividend paid by a company liable to Malta tax = the **gross amount** before tax deducted; where no deduction was made, the dividend is grossed up by the tax charged on the underlying profits (statutory basis of full imputation).

## 15. Final-Tax Rental Regimes (Arts 31A–31E) — FTA allocation
- **31A:** rent to Housing Authority ≥10 years: **5% final** withheld.
- **31B:** rent to subsidised tenant: **10% final**.
- **31C:** restored UCA/scheduled property rents: **10%** residential / **15%** commercial, remit by 30 June following year.
- **31D — Optional 15% final tax on rents:** 15c per euro of **gross** rental income from tenements (residential; commercial only if not rented to/from related body >25%); option covers ALL tenements for the year; final — no set-off/refund; **(5)** undeclared rent discovered on enquiry: **35% of gross, final, plus interest and additional tax**; **(6) profits → FTA**.
- **31E:** ≥7-year Housing Authority scheme rents: **5% final**.

## 16. Investment Income Provisions (Arts 32–41A)
- **Art 33:** payor deducts **15%** from investment income paid to a "recipient"; for Art 41(a)(viii) income (refund-registered shareholder dividends/refunds, deemed distributions) deduction at the **35% rate** or prescribed rate. Remit by 14th day after month-end; annual account by **31 January**; certificate to recipient. Fine up to €23,000.
- **Art 35:** election to be paid gross (not for 41(a)(viii)(2)–(4) income).
- **Art 37:** tax withheld under 33(1) is **not creditable or refundable**.
- **Art 38–39:** final withholding; no further disclosure/tax for individuals.
- **Art 41 definitions:** "investment income" includes Malta bank interest, government/corporate public issue interest, CIS redemption gains, **(viii)(2) the net dividend paid to an ITMA 48(4)/(4A) refund-registered shareholder, and (viii)(3) the refund itself** (payor = the Commissioner). "Recipient" = Malta-resident persons (with bank/insurance/company exclusions).
- **Art 41A:** CIS prescribed-fund rules; authorised financial intermediary definitions; CIS cannot elect gross payment.

# Malta Income Tax Act (CAP. 123) — Part 3 Digest (pp. 147–218, Arts. 41B–96 + Schedule)
Scope note: shareholder refunds ("6/7ths" etc.) are governed by **article 48(4)/(4A) of the Income Tax MANAGEMENT Act (Cap. 372)**, not CAP 123 art. 48. This part of CAP 123 repeatedly cross-references ITMA art. 48(4)/(4A); CAP 123 art. 48 itself deals with trading stock.

## 1. Investment income provisions — tail end and anti-avoidance

### Art. 41A(h) (fragment at start of file)
- In no case shall a refund be made to a collective investment scheme (CIS) in respect of tax withheld under the investment income provisions from investment income paid to that CIS.

### Art. 41B — Ministerial power
- Minister may make regulations determining how the investment income provisions apply to particular types/categories of securities, with modifications as deemed necessary.

### Art. 42 — Anti-avoidance (investment income provisions)
- Where, in the Commissioner's opinion, a **series of transactions** is effected with the **sole or main purpose** of reducing tax by reason of the operation of the investment income provisions, the person is assessable as if those provisions did not apply; any tax withheld on income from such transactions is available as a **credit** against the tax liability of the person receiving the income, or for a refund, for the relevant year of assessment.
- "Series of transactions" = any two or more corresponding or circular transactions carried out by the same person, directly or indirectly.

## 2. Deemed distributions and shareholder-level deeming rules

### Art. 43 — Undistributed profits deemed distributed
- (1) Where the Commissioner is of the opinion that a company (other than a company incorporated/registered outside Malta and not resident in Malta) has not distributed profits and the effect of non-distribution is **avoidance or reduction of tax otherwise payable by the shareholders**, he may by written notice order that undistributed profits (or part) be deemed distributed as dividend, in such amount and on such date(s) as appears reasonable; shareholders assessed accordingly.
- Proviso (a): no order if the company proves the main purpose (or one of the main purposes) of non-distribution was (i) development/expansion of its trade/business from internal sources (alone or combined with other sources), or (ii) repayment of a loan/overdraft/capital borrowed from external sources (not directly or indirectly linked to the company's shareholders) used in expansion of the trade/business.
- Proviso (b)(iii): no order effective if made **after the lapse of 8 years** from the end of the year of assessment in which the profits were chargeable (older transitional cut-offs for pre-1976 and pre-1984 profits).
- Proviso (c): where the deemed dividend recipient is itself a company ("second company"), the dividend is NOT chargeable as income of that company but is treated as onward-distributed by it on the date determined by the Commissioner, cascading mutatis mutandis until no part remains treated as distributed to a company.
- (2) If a shareholder assessed under (1) fails to pay on due date, the tax becomes a **debt due to Government from the company** and recoverable as such.
- (3) Deemed-distributed profits subsequently actually distributed are NOT taxable income in the recipients' hands.
- (4) Within **21 days** of service of the deeming notice, the company must furnish each shareholder with a certificate showing the deemed dividend amount and the tax the company would have been entitled to deduct under art. 59 had the dividend been paid.
- (5) Commissioner's decision may be questioned by objection/appeal under arts. 35 and 37 ITMA (Cap. 372).
- (6) Deemed dividends for individuals connected to refund-registered persons:
  - (a) A Malta-resident individual registered for ITMA art. 48(4)/(4A) purposes (or beneficially entitled, directly/indirectly, to the profits of a registered person/entity) is **deemed to receive a dividend** equal to the profits (net of tax paid/payable by the company) he is beneficially entitled to receive, on the **first day of the accounting period following** that in which the profits were earned or on the date of actual distribution, whichever is earlier. Not applicable where the profits were actually distributed to him by way of dividend in the accounting period in which earned. Where deemed received on the first day of the following period, the dividend is deemed paid on that day for art. 41(a)(viii)(2) purposes.
  - (b) A Malta-resident individual beneficially entitled (directly/indirectly) to profits of a person/entity that has received income referred to in art. 41(a)(viii)(3) is deemed to receive, when that person/entity receives it, so much as corresponds to his entitlement; carve-out where distributable profits have been deemed income under art. 41(a)(viii)(4) paid to a "recipient".
  - (d)(i) Where an individual **becomes** beneficially entitled (or entitlement is increased) to profits of a person/entity that has received but not distributed (or is entitled to receive) income in art. 41(a)(viii)(2) and (3), he is deemed to receive a chargeable dividend at that time and must declare it in his return — unless the Commissioner is satisfied the events were not the result of an arrangement/scheme whose sole or main purpose was avoidance/postponement of tax.
  - (d)(ii) Non-resident individual who becomes resident and domiciled in Malta while so entitled: deemed dividend at the time of becoming resident and domiciled, must be declared — not applicable if he was not resident in Malta at any time throughout the whole **5 years** immediately preceding the change.
  - (d)(iii) Individual not resident in Malta who receives such income and was resident and domiciled in Malta at any time within the **5-year period** preceding receipt: income chargeable and must be declared.
  - (e) Where the immediate shareholder is not an individual and not resident in Malta, paras (a)/(b) apply only when the individual is **ordinarily resident and domiciled in Malta**.
  - (f) Commissioner's absolute discretion to disapply the sub-article for public companies/entities whose shares are listed on a recognised stock exchange and widely held and frequently traded (or, though not listed/frequently traded, widely held), where Malta ordinarily-resident-and-domiciled individuals do not own/control a substantial part.
  - (g) "entity" = person other than individual, including any trust or body of persons.

### Art. 44 — Special provisions for deemed distributed profits (historic)
- Company may apply in writing to have art. 43 deemed-distributed profits deemed distributed as: (a) 20% on the date(s) the Commissioner ordered; (b) 80% in the year immediately preceding year of assessment 1991. Application invalid after 30 June 1991 (or 30 days from service of the deeming order, if later, for the sub-article (2) case).
- (3) Additional tax charged on the company on the profits deemed distributed: **25c per euro** for years preceding YA 1990; **0.025c per euro** for YA 1991.
- (4) On subsequent actual distribution: dividend grossed up per art. 31 **without** the sub-article (3) additional tax; that additional tax is **not** available for set-off under art. 60.

### Art. 45 — Historic distributions
- Distributions made after 1 January 1990 out of profits of years preceding YA 1989 are treated as NOT distributed when determining undistributed profits for art. 43.

### Art. 45A — Conversion of a company into a commercial partnership
Where a company converts into a partnership en nom collectif / en commandite that does not elect to be treated as a company (ITMA art. 27(6)), or a partnership/EEIG ceases its election to be a company:
- (a) Any balance of distributable profits **allocated to any of the tax accounts** existing on the day the company ceases to be a company is **deemed distributed by way of dividend on that day**; arts. 61–67 apply accordingly (i.e. untaxed-account 15% withholding etc.).
- (b) Otherwise no transfer/acquisition of assets is deemed to occur; the partnership inherits the company's cost and date of acquisition.
- (c)/(d) Where assets include shares or Malta immovable property acquired intra-group with relief under arts. 5(9)/5A(4)(f): deemed degrouping for arts. 5(9A)/5A(12A); special rule where the transferor is converted.
- (e) Capital losses (art. 5(10)) carried into the partnership, set off only against its capital gains as would have applied absent the conversion.
- (f) Trading losses (art. 14(1)(g)) similarly carried forward against the partnership's total income.

### Art. 45B — Conversion of a partnership into a company
- On conversion of an en nom collectif / en commandite partnership into a company (or election under ITMA art. 27(6)), no transfer/acquisition of assets is deemed; the company inherits the partnership's cost and date of acquisition.

### Art. 46 — Loans to shareholders
- (1) Advances, loans, distributions of assets, or payments made by a company on behalf of / for the individual benefit of shareholders are, to the extent the Commissioner considers they represent a distribution of income, **deemed dividends** paid out of profits.
- (2) Where in a subsequent year the company sets off an actual dividend against the advance/loan/payment, that dividend, to that extent, is deemed NOT a dividend.

### Art. 47 — Liquidation distributions and untaxed account distributions
- Distributions by a liquidator in winding up (of a company or partnership), to the extent they represent income derived by the company/partnership (before or during liquidation), **and any distributions from the untaxed account**, are deemed dividends paid out of profits (or profits distributed to partners).

## 3. Trading stock and miscellaneous computation rules

### Art. 48 — Trading stock sold with a business
- (1) Where trading stock is sold together with other business assets, the Commissioner determines the part of the consideration attributable to trading stock; that amount is deemed the purchaser's price.
- (2) Stock disposed of otherwise than by sale, or sold for non-cash consideration, is deemed to realise the **market price** of the day of disposal/sale; absent a market price, the price the Commissioner determines.
- (3) Right of appeal preserved (ITMA arts. 35, 37).

### Art. 48A / 48B (peripheral)
- 48A: rules may regulate tax on handmade outwork (knitting, lace, crochet, embroidery) paid by a VAT-registered person where net remuneration ≤ €500/year.
- 48B: Minister may prescribe how art. 4(1)(a) (trade) chargeable income of prescribed persons is calculated/estimated.

## 4. General anti-avoidance, transfer pricing, rulings

### Art. 51 — Tax avoidance (GAAR)
- (1) Artificial or fictitious schemes, or schemes not in fact given effect to, which reduce tax: Commissioner shall disregard the scheme; person assessed accordingly.
- (2)(a) Where a person, as a direct or indirect result of any scheme whose **sole or main purpose** was obtaining an advantage (avoiding, reducing, or postponing tax, or obtaining any refund or set-off of tax), has obtained or is in a position to obtain the advantage, the Commissioner shall by written order determine that person's (or any other person's) liability/entitlement so as to nullify or modify the scheme. Objection and appeal rights as if the order were an ITMA assessment.
- (2)(b) **Parent–Subsidiary Directive GAAR** (implements Directive 2015/121 amending 2011/96/EU): Directive benefits denied to arrangements (or series) put in place for the main purpose or one of the main purposes of obtaining a tax advantage defeating the Directive's object, which are not genuine having regard to all facts; "not genuine" = not put in place for valid commercial reasons reflecting economic reality; applies step-by-step; without prejudice to other anti-abuse provisions.
- (3) Income payable to an unmarried child under 18 as a result of a disposition is treated as the disponer's income.
- (4) **Loss/allowance trafficking on change in shareholding**: where income is received/accrued to a company as a direct/indirect result of a scheme or a change in shareholding, then unless it is proved the scheme/change was NOT entered into solely or mainly to obtain the benefit of losses or balances of losses, or wear-and-tear/initial allowances or balances: (a) arts. 5(10)(b) and 14(1)(g) (loss carry-forward) do not apply to losses of the year of the scheme/change or carried into or from that year; (b) second proviso to art. 14(1)(f) does not allow carry-forward of capital allowances (14(1)(f) and (j)); (c) art. 24 (balancing) applied as though the above had not taken effect.
- (5) "scheme" includes any disposition, agreement, arrangement, trust, grant, covenant, transfer of assets, **increase in share capital of a company** and alienation of property, irrespective of date.

### Art. 51A — Transfer pricing
- Minister may make rules on transfer pricing generally: determination of **arm's length pricing** of a transaction or series, adjustments, and **advance pricing agreements**.

### Art. 52 — Advance Revenue Rulings
- (1) On application by a company party to a transaction: ruling that **art. 51 will not apply**, provided the Commissioner is satisfied the transaction is for bona fide commercial reasons.
- (2) Ruling that paragraph (f) of the "participating holding" definition (art. 2) applies to a particular (or to-be-acquired) shareholding.
- (3) Ruling on tax treatment of transactions concerning any financial instrument or other security (applicant must be a company).
- (4) Ruling on tax treatment of transactions involving international business (Commissioner's discretion on what constitutes international business).
- (5) Ruling whether a company qualifies as an international trading company — none for companies registered in Malta on/after 1 Jan 2007 not resident before that date; companies registered 18 Apr–31 Dec 2006 had to apply by 30 June 2007. No art. 52(5) ruling effective on/after 1 Jan 2011.
- (5A) Ruling that a company is not precluded from being an ITMA art. 48(4A)(b) company by paragraph (ii) thereof; ruling remains binding only if with each income tax return the company also files: (a) a **directors' declaration** (or company-secretary declaration approved by the board, with stated details of the board meeting and voting) confirming activities throughout the accounting period consisted solely of those in the application; and (b) an **auditor's declaration** confirming the directors' declaration is correct to the best of his knowledge and belief.
- (7) Applications in writing with all material particulars; Commissioner may require further particulars within 30 days; ruling notified within **30 days** of application (or of compliance with a further-particulars notice). Rulings are **void** if particulars did not fully and accurately disclose all material facts.
- (8) Ruling binding for **2 years from any relevant change in statutory provisions** or **5 years from the ruling, whichever is the lesser**; renewable at the applicant's option for a further 5 years (renewal not to be unreasonably withheld; application must state material changes to facts).

### Art. 52A — Finance leasing
- Minister may make rules on finance leasing: deductions allowable to lessor and lessee, and the manner/extent to which losses from finance leasing may be set off against gains from other sources.

### Art. 52B — EU Directives and Regulations
- Minister may by regulations implement any EU Directive/Regulation affecting the Income Tax Acts; such regulations prevail notwithstanding anything to the contrary in the Income Tax Acts.

## 5. Rates of tax (Art. 56) — company-relevant provisions

### Art. 56(6) — Standard company rate
- Tax charged at **35 cents (0.35) on every euro** of the chargeable income of every: (a) **company**; (b) body corporate established by law; (c) undertaking required by art. 30(7)(d) to be treated as a separate body of persons.

### Art. 56(4)–(5) — 20% entities
- **20 cents (0.20) per euro** on chargeable income of: (a) every entity to which art. 30 applies; (b) any foundation, bequest, trust, institution or organisation whose income is specifically due to be wholly applied to providing income to members of the clergy (with exemption for parts actually so applied to Malta-resident clergy/ecclesiastical communities). (5) Tax charged under this rate is **not** part of tax available for set-off for collection under art. 60.

### Art. 56(8) — International trading company dividends
- A person receiving a dividend distributed by an international trading company out of profits derived while it was an ITC is taxed at **27.5%** on the dividend as separate chargeable income, where the person is: (a) non-resident and not owned/controlled by (nor acting for) Malta ordinarily-resident-and-domiciled individuals; or (b) a Malta-resident company wholly owned by non-residents (with same ownership condition). Transitional: 1 Jan 2011–31 Dec 2014 for companies that were ITCs at 31 Dec 2010; conditions (a)/(b) disapplied for dividends to shareholders registered under ITMA art. 48(4)/(4A).

### Art. 56(9) — Maximum rates in certain cases
- (a) Trade union: max **10c** per euro. (b) Club or similar non-proprietary institution (no income distributable to/available for proprietors or members): max **30c** per euro. (c) Transfers of property in art. 31C(1) circumstances: max **30c** per euro, deemed the last part of total income.

### Art. 56(12) — Additional (penalty) tax for default/omission — applies to companies
- (a) Default in furnishing a return (pre-YA 1999): treble tax (or lesser as determined; minimum the greater of €23 or 0.5% of tax due).
- (b) Omission from a return (pre-YA 1999): additional tax equal to **twice the difference** between tax on returned income and tax properly chargeable.
- (c) Default in furnishing a return, or omission from a return, for **YA 1999 onward** (or default in furnishing any form required under the Income Tax Acts): additional tax per the **Schedule** (see §12 below), payable in addition to the tax properly chargeable.
- (d) Additional tax under (c) suspended for the period between initiation and conclusion of a **Mutual Agreement Procedure** (art. 76 arrangements incl. EU Arbitration Convention 90/436/EEC).
- Proviso: if the Commissioner is satisfied the default/omission was not due to fraud, art, contrivance or gross or wilful neglect, he **shall remit** the whole treble/additional tax under (a)/(b); otherwise may remit part/all. For a **body of persons**, the Commissioner shall not reduce tax under (a) below the greater of €2 or 10% of the total tax chargeable for the year.
- ITMA art. 12(3) notice cases: additional tax as specified in the notice, capped at **€23** per notice; remittable only for absence from Malta, sickness or other reasonable cause.
- Deemed omission: deducting/setting off any amount not allowed, showing expenditure/loss not actually incurred, or providing/withholding information resulting in reduced tax payable or increased repayment.
- Additional tax under this sub-article is NOT part of tax paid/payable for arts. 59, 76, 89 or ITMA arts. 51, 52 (i.e. **cannot be imputed to dividends or refunded**).
- (iv) Where the default/omission is in a return required to be furnished by another person **on behalf of a company**, the company itself is liable for the additional tax.

### Art. 56(13) — Contractors
- (a) Tax on chargeable income of a "Contractor" (art. 23) at **35c per euro** where computed under art. 23(1)-(2); other income at appropriate rates. (b) Withholding by a Contractor from payments to a **sub-contractor** (art. 23(5)): **10c per euro** of payments.

### Art. 56(18) / (18A) — Final 10% charges
- (18) Tax on art. 4(6) income at **10c per euro**; no set-off or refund of tax so charged.
- (18A) Non-resident deriving income from **entertainment activities exercised in Malta ≤15 days** in the year: **10% of the gross payment**, no set-off/refund. If >15 days: must declare in a return, charged at art. 56(1)(c) non-resident rates, with the 10% creditable/refundable.

### Art. 56(19) — Administrative easements
- Minister may direct that small assessments below a specified amount not be raised, and that the Commissioner may round amounts to the nearest euro in determining chargeable income, tax due and set-offs.

### Art. 56(20) — Treaty-based reduced rate elected at company level
- (a) Where a member of a Malta-resident company is resident in a treaty State and under the treaty a dividend is subject to Malta tax at a rate lower than that chargeable on the underlying income, the company may **require that its trading gains/profits distributable as such dividends (YA 2001 onwards) be taxed at the reduced treaty rate** instead of the normal rate, even before distribution.
  - Only for companies which **do not sell by retail**; deemed not retail where sales are to (i) persons carrying on a trade/business/profession/vocation who resell or use the goods/services for that purpose, or (ii) non-individual persons using the goods for an undertaking.
  - Where the option is exercised, **no refund** under the Income Tax Acts may be claimed on dividends paid out of profits taxed at the reduced rate, other than a refund under ITMA art. 48(4A).
  - Where the company distributes normally-taxed profits to the treaty-resident member: that member cannot claim any refund on such dividends.
  - Where reduced-rate profits are distributed to a person NOT entitled to the reduced treaty rate: those profits are taxed at the **difference between the art. 56(6) rate and the rate actually applied**, payable by the company in the year of assessment of the distribution.
- (b) On a change in shareholding where new shareholders are not entitled (or entitled to a higher treaty rate): undistributed reduced-rate profits at end of the last financial year preceding the change (less such profits distributed to the outgoing shareholder in the current year) taxed at the difference between the rate that would have applied to the new shareholder and the rate actually applied; payable by the company in the year of assessment of distribution. Look-through where the member is a Maltese-incorporated company.

### Art. 56(1)–(2), (10)–(11), (17), (21)–(27) — individual rates (context for withholding/FSS and shareholder-level effects)
- 56(1)(a)/(b): progressive individual resident rates (married/single/parent), 0%/15%/25%/35% bands, top rate 35% above €60,000 (band thresholds as amended, some applicable YA 2026/2027).
- 56(1)(c): non-resident individuals: 0% first €700; 20% next €2,400; 30% next €4,700; 35% remainder — with EU/EEA 90%-of-worldwide-income provisos.
- 56(1)(d): **any other person** (non-resident body etc.): 15% first €2,400; 20% next €2,400; 30% next €3,500; 35% remainder.
- 56(17): 15% on qualifying employment mainly outside Malta (conditions: contract ≥12 months; ≤30 days presence in Malta, exclusions).
- 56(21): 15% option for qualifying contracts of employment (highly qualified persons), with prescribed minimum income.
- 56(26)/(26A): 7.5% final tax on sports/artistic income of individuals.
- 56(27): €5,000 minimum tax for non-domiciled residents with foreign income ≥€35,000 not fully received in Malta.

## 6. Dividend imputation machinery, tax accounts, distributions (Arts. 59–69) — CORE for company returns

### Art. 59 — Deduction of tax from dividends (imputation)
- (1)(a) Every **company registered in Malta** (other than a CIS) is entitled to deduct from any dividend — **other than a dividend paid out of distributable profits allocated to the untaxed account** — tax **at the rate paid or payable by the company** (double taxation relief left out of account) on the income out of which the dividend is paid. Where tax is not paid/payable on the whole income, the deduction is restricted to the portion paid out of income on which tax is paid/payable.
- (1)(b) CIS: may deduct at the rate paid, payable or **suffered** by the scheme, whichever is highest (same restriction); art. 31 (gross-up) applies to the recipient.
- (2) Under-deduction after a rate increase: company may (a) make up the under-deduction from the **next dividend** (regardless of whether the same person is entitled), or (b) with the Commissioner's written permission, recover from the person paid the original dividend as a debt due to the company.
- (3)–(4) **Over-deduction**: company must make good via reduced deduction from the next dividend, unless it has paid the over-deduction to the Commissioner; company must render an account of the over-deduction to the Commissioner **within 14 days from the tax return date** relevant to the income out of which the dividend was paid; the Commissioner may by notice require payment (debt due to Government, payable within one month of service).
- (5) **Dividend certificate — mandatory on every dividend payment** (whether or not tax deducted). For dividends paid out of profits of the accounting period in which paid, the certificate may be furnished after period-end but **not later than the tax return date** of the relative year of assessment. Certificate (in the form the Commissioner requires) must show per shareholder:
  - (i) gross taxed amount of distributed profits **per taxed account**, before company tax;
  - (ii) total tax chargeable on the company on the distributed profits, showing separately: (1) Malta tax payable after all double-tax reliefs and tax credits; (2) foreign tax relieved under treaty/Commonwealth/unilateral relief; (3) amount set off under the **flat-rate foreign tax credit**; (4) amount and description of tax credits deducted;
  - (iii) distributed profits from each taxed account net of the above tax;
  - (iv) tax payable **on distribution** under arts. 62, 67 and 67A, shown separately;
  - (v) net dividend paid;
  - (vi) the "**net Malta rate**" where company tax is affected by double taxation relief;
  - (vii) where exempt profits are distributed and exempt in the shareholder's hands, a note quoting the exempting law;
  - (viii) where distributed profits include a dividend from another company in respect of which the distributing company can claim an **ITMA art. 48(4) refund**, a statement declaring entitlement;
  - (ix) analysis distinguishing profits chargeable in **YA 2007 and earlier** vs **YA 2008 onward** (per year of assessment);
  - (x) other information the Commissioner requires.
- (8) Accounts/certificates rendered by the manager or other principal officer of the company.
- (9) For arts. 59–60: "company" includes a CIS; "dividend" includes CIS distributions; "relief of double taxation" = credits/reliefs under art. 74; "shareholder" includes unit-holders; "tax chargeable on the company" includes tax suffered by a CIS.

### Art. 60 — Set-off of dividend tax (imputation credit)
- Tax deducted (or deductible) under art. 59 from a dividend, debenture interest or other loan interest, and tax applicable to a share of a body of persons' income, is **set off for collection** against the tax charged on the recipient's chargeable income when included therein.
- Where tax was actually deducted, the amount actually deducted is set off irrespective of under/over-deduction adjustments — except where the company recovered an under-deduction under 59(2)(b), in which case both the deducted and recovered amounts are set off.
- **No set-off unless the art. 59(5) certificate is produced** to the Commissioner **and tax has actually been paid**.
- No set-off for: (a) tax deductible from dividends paid to persons exempt from tax on them; (b) tax charged under art. 56(4) or ITMA art. 27(3)-(4).

### Art. 61 — Definitions for arts. 62–69
- "**recipient**": (i) a person, other than a company, resident in Malta in the year the dividend is received (by him or on his behalf); (ii) a non-resident person (including a non-resident company) owned and controlled by, directly or indirectly, or acting on behalf of, an individual ordinarily resident and domiciled in Malta; (iii) a trustee of a trust whose beneficiaries are persons in (i)/(ii); (iv) an EU/EEA individual (and spouse) in the art. 56(1)(c) proviso circumstances.
- "**untaxed dividend**": a dividend paid by a Malta-resident company to the extent paid out of distributable profits allocated to its **untaxed account**.

### Art. 62 — 15% withholding on untaxed account dividends
- (1) Every company shall, on payment of an untaxed dividend **to a recipient**, deduct tax at **15%**.
- (2) Amount deducted is a debt due to the Commissioner **payable not later than the 14th day following the end of the month** in which the dividend was paid. Not applicable to untaxed dividends paid to persons **exempt from tax**.

### Arts. 63–66 — Presumption / disclosure / election
- **63**: For the recipient's liability, deduction and payment which ought to have been made under art. 62 are presumed made.
- **64**: Where the presumption applies: recipient need not disclose the dividend in any return; no further tax chargeable (subject to art. 65).
- **65**: Recipient may **opt to declare** the untaxed dividend in his return; tax/repayment then determined as if art. 64 did not apply, with the 15% credited and refundable where applicable.
- **66**: Untaxed dividend paid to a **non-recipient** (as defined in art. 61): not charged to tax in that person's hands; a non-resident non-recipient need not disclose it in any return.

### Art. 67 — Distributions out of IPA / MTA / FIA taxed at historic lower rates
- (1) Where a company pays a dividend to a person resident in Malta out of profits allocated to the **immovable property account, Maltese taxed account or foreign income account** which suffered tax at a rate lower than the art. 56(6) rate in force at distribution **only because a different rate applied when the profits were charged**, the company shall deduct tax equal to the **difference between the current rate and the rate actually suffered**, on the dividend amount before deducting company tax suffered. Not applicable to dividends to persons exempt from tax.
- (2) Deduction is a debt due to the Commissioner **payable by the 14th day following the end of the month** of payment.
- (3) Arts. 63–65 (presumption/no-disclosure/option to declare) apply mutatis mutandis.
- (4) "Person resident in Malta" here includes a non-resident (including non-resident company) owned/controlled by or acting for a Malta ordinarily-resident-and-domiciled individual.

### Art. 67A — Collective investment schemes
- (1) A CIS allocates **no profits to the foreign income account**; dividends received out of another company's FIA are allocated to the **Maltese taxed account** (without prejudice to final tax account and immovable property account allocations).
- (2)–(4) Non-corporate CIS distributing to Malta residents: deduct tax as if it were a company, on distributions that would have been allocated to the untaxed account; payable by the 14th day following month-end; arts. 63–65 apply; exempt persons excluded.
- (6)–(7) An **equalisation reserve** forms part of distributable profits; "dividend" includes amounts distributed out of an equalisation reserve (definition given).

### Art. 68 — Disclosure, final tax account, ordering rules, deemed distributions to refund-registered shareholders
- (1)(a) Non-residents, and Malta-resident individuals, receiving dividends out of profits allocated to any of the **taxed accounts other than the final tax account**: no obligation to disclose in any return. (b) No further tax on such income.
- (1)(c) Dividends paid out of the **final tax account**: not charged to further tax, do **not** form part of anyone's chargeable income, and **no credit or refund may be claimed** for tax directly or indirectly paid on those profits. Look-through: a dividend from a non-Malta company is, to the extent it directly/indirectly comprises an FTA dividend of a Malta company, deemed an FTA dividend paid directly to the recipient.
- (2) Non-corporate CIS distributions that would have been FIA/MTA profits: no disclosure by persons other than Malta-resident companies; no further tax.
- (3) **Ordering rule**: a dividend must be paid out of profits allocated to the **immovable property account before** any profits allocated to the Maltese taxed account are distributed.
- (4) For companies resident in Malta before 1 Jan 2007: MTA dividends deemed paid out of profits earned in accounting periods commencing **prior to 1 Jan 2011** first (FIFO), and only when wholly distributed are later profits considered distributed; any dividend paid on/after 1 Jan 2007 not involving actual cash payment is **ignored and deemed never made** for this purpose.
- (5) Where a person is **registered under ITMA art. 48(4A)** to claim refunds and the company's MTA or FIA profits are actually or deemed distributed, that person is **deemed to receive from each such account** the proportion corresponding to his percentage entitlement to participate in distributions. **Memorandum/articles or agreement provisions directing that a registered shareholder be paid dividends solely/mainly from the MTA or FIA are disregarded.** Proviso: does not apply to profits taxed under art. 15 Business Promotion Act or art. 56(20); unless the original shares no longer exist, such profits are distributable only to the person to whom those articles applied (or his acquirer).

### Art. 69 — Failure to deduct/pay distribution tax
- Failure to deduct and pay tax under arts. 62, 67, 67A: art. 73(4) (charge of the tax plus **twice the amount** as additional tax, recoverable, notice as evidence, discretionary remission, non-recoverable from payee, not creditable) and ITMA art. 40(1) apply mutatis mutandis.

## 7. Persons assessable / representatives

### Art. 70 — Receivers, administrators, etc.
- A receiver, administrator, guardian, tutor, curator, judicial sequestrator or committee managing property/concerns on behalf of a person is chargeable to tax on the income as that person would be, and is answerable for all determination, assessment and payment obligations; person represented remains liable in his own name. (2) Managers/administrators of art. 30 property or art. 56(3)(b) bodies deemed administrators.

### Art. 71 — Deceased persons
- Heirs/legal representatives liable for tax of a deceased individual (death in the year preceding assessment, or during the year of assessment or within **5 years** after) and jointly and severally answerable; if estate distributed before the year of assessment begins, tax paid at rates in force at death.

### Art. 73 — Withholding on income paid to non-residents
- (1) Any person paying income chargeable under the Act to a non-resident (or to a resident on a non-resident's behalf), unless himself liable under ITMA art. 5, must deduct tax:
  - (a) **25c per euro** for non-resident persons other than companies (and other than art. 56(18A) entertainers);
  - (b) at the **art. 56(6) rate (35%)** for non-resident companies;
  - (c) at the art. 56(18A) rate (10% gross) for short-stay entertainers.
  - Commissioner may authorise by written notice a lower rate or no deduction. NOT applicable to income already subjected to deduction under art. 59 or ITMA art. 23 (FSS).
- (2) Deducted tax is a debt due to Government by the payer, payable/remitted within **30 days** of the deduction.
- (3) Deductions under (1)(a)/(b), once paid, are set off against tax charged on the non-resident; any excess refunded under ITMA art. 48.
- (4) Failure to deduct or to pay over: payer chargeable with the tax **plus twice the amount of the tax**; recoverable as tax; Commissioner's notice is prima facie evidence; remission discretionary; the additional tax is borne by the payer and not recoverable from the payee; not deemed tax paid/payable for arts. 59, 76, 89 and ITMA arts. 42, 51, 52.

## 8. Relief of double taxation (Arts. 74–95)

### Art. 74 — Four reliefs
- (a) double taxation (treaty) relief (arts. 76–78); (b) unilateral relief (arts. 79–88); (c) Commonwealth income tax relief (art. 89); (d) **flat-rate foreign tax credit (FRFTC)** (arts. 92–95).

### Art. 75 — Ordering of reliefs
- Unilateral relief applies only where treaty relief and Commonwealth relief are not available; the FRFTC applies only where treaty relief, Commonwealth relief AND unilateral relief are all not available.

### Art. 76 — Treaty arrangements
- Ministerial order gives effect to arrangements for double taxation relief, prevention of fiscal evasion, and assistance in collection, notwithstanding anything in any enactment; rules may govern exchange of information mechanics.

### Art. 77 — Treaty credit mechanics
- (2) Income tax reduced by the credit; credit only if the person entitled to the income is **resident in Malta** in the year preceding the year of assessment.
- (3) Per-source cap: credit ≤ the Malta tax computed at the **average effective rate** (income tax chargeable before credit ÷ total income) applied to the doubly-taxed income (computed under the Act).
- (4) Overall cap: total credits under all arrangements ≤ total income tax payable for the year **less** tax payable under arts. 40, 69 and 73.
- (5) Computation: (a) no deduction for the foreign tax; (b) remittance-based income grossed up by the foreign tax; (c) gross-up for underlying tax where the arrangement provides.
- (7) Where the arrangement provides underlying-tax credit for some dividend classes only, a dividend paid to a company controlling directly or indirectly **not less than one-half of the voting power** of the payer is treated as if of a class benefiting from underlying-tax credit.
- (8) Election possible for credit not to be allowed.
- (9) **Claims within 2 years after the end of the year of assessment**; disputes subject to objection and appeal like an assessment.
- (10) Where foreign/Malta tax adjustments render a credit excessive/insufficient: time limits disapplied for consequential returns/assessments/claims made within 2 years of all material determinations.

### Art. 78 — Dividends where company tax affected by double taxation relief
- (1) Where the company's tax is affected by double taxation relief, the amount to be **set off under art. 60 or repaid under ITMA art. 48** in respect of tax deductible from a dividend is reduced: (a) if no tax chargeable on the recipient on the dividend — by tax on the gross dividend at the "rate of double taxation relief"; (b) if the recipient's rate is below the rate of double taxation relief — by tax on the gross dividend at the difference.
- (2) Such dividends deemed the **highest part** of total income; dividends with lower net Malta rate ranked higher; parts taxed at different rates treated as separate dividends; "rate of double taxation relief" = excess of the rate deductible from the dividend over the **net Malta rate**.

### Arts. 79–88 — Unilateral relief
- **80**: available where Malta tax is computed on income that (a) arises outside Malta and (b) is subject to foreign tax of a similar character (including sub-national/municipal taxes; and including, for art. 82(a)-(b) underlying-tax claims, tax imposed under the Income Tax Acts).
- **81**: credit against Malta tax; only if the person is **resident in Malta or a company registered in Malta** for the year preceding the year of assessment.
- **82**: **underlying tax credit** for dividends from a non-resident ("overseas") company: tax not chargeable directly or by deduction (i.e. corporate tax on profits) is creditable; extends along a **chain of successive dividends** between related companies, where "related" = the overseas company controls, directly or indirectly, **not less than 10% of the voting power** of the related company.
- **83**: per-source cap identical in structure to art. 77(3).
- **84**: overall cap: total unilateral credits ≤ total income tax payable less tax payable under arts. 40, 69, 73.
- **85–86**: computation rules (no deduction for the foreign tax; gross-up of remittances; gross-up by underlying-tax credit amount).
- **87**: art. 77(8),(9),(10) and art. 78 apply mutatis mutandis.
- **88**: **proof requirement** — no unilateral relief unless the person proves to the Commissioner's satisfaction that the income bore the foreign tax and proves the amount of that tax.

### Art. 89 — Commonwealth relief
- Resident: relief at the Commonwealth rate if that rate ≤ half the Malta rate; otherwise at half the Malta rate. Non-resident: half the Commonwealth rate if Commonwealth rate ≤ Malta rate; otherwise the excess of the Malta rate over half the Commonwealth rate. Requires reciprocal relief in the other country; claims within **2 years** after the end of the year of assessment; not applicable to art. 23 (contractor) profits.

### Art. 91 — Precedence
- Where more than one "last part of total income" rule applies: art. 78 has precedence over art. 56, and both over art. 90.

### Arts. 92–95 — Flat-Rate Foreign Tax Credit (FRFTC)
- **92(1)**: FRFTC available for income/gains (a) received by a **company registered in Malta**, (b) which the company is empowered to receive and which fall to be **allocated to the foreign income account** (excluding dividends paid out of the FIA of another Malta-resident company), and (c) supported by documentary evidence satisfactory to the Commissioner that the income falls to be allocated to the FIA — a **certificate by a certified public accountant and auditor** is satisfactory evidence.
- **92(2)**: same for a "**Rule 9 company**" (a company that elected under rule 9 of the Tax Accounts (Income Tax) Rules, S.L. 123.101, not to allocate profits to FIA/MTA) — tested as if it were not a Rule 9 company.
- **92(3)**: where FRFTC is claimed together with an art. 14(1)(o) deduction, the art. 94(1) gross-up amount is ignored in computing any chargeable-income-based cap on the (o) deduction.
- **93**: FRFTC = **25% of the income or gains receivable**, before any deductions or payments; for dividends, capital gains, interest, royalties, rents and other income from investments situated outside Malta, computed on the amount receivable **after deducting foreign tax** (direct or withholding) but before any other deductions.
- **94(1)**: the credit is **added to (grossed up into) the income**; the aggregate is the amount chargeable.
- **94(2)**: tax payable reduced by the FRFTC; **cap**: where the FRFTC exceeds **85% of the tax payable** (computed on the profits to be allocated to the FIA, after deducting foreign tax set off under treaty/Commonwealth/unilateral relief), the excess is NOT available for set-off or refund for any purpose. [Effective ceiling: max effective FRFTC relief = 85% of the Malta tax on FIA profits.]
- **94(3)**: art. 77(8)-(10) and art. 78 apply mutatis mutandis.
- **95**: anti-avoidance — a **series of transactions** (two or more corresponding or circular transactions by the same person, directly or indirectly) with the sole or main purpose of reducing tax via the FRFTC: assessed as if the FRFTC provisions did not apply.

## 9. Bearer accounts and part-time/overtime (peripheral to companies)
- **Art. 90**: Bearer Accounts Levy paid reduces tax chargeable on interest from the account; that interest deemed highest part of total income.
- **Art. 90A**: part-time work of individuals — final tax originally 15%, reduced to **10% from YA 2023** (90A(11)); employer paying part-time 4(1)(b) income must **withhold at the applicable rate** and remit (90A(7)); employer remains liable under ITMA art. 23(4) for failures (90A(6)).
- **Art. 90B**: qualifying overtime income taxed at **15%**, final unless the individual elects otherwise (YA 2021 onward; limits prescribed by rules).

## 10. Rule-making and force of guidelines (Art. 96)
- (1) Minister may make rules for carrying out the Act.
- (2) **Guidelines, explanations or instructions published by/under the Commissioner's authority** and made available to taxpayers generally are read as one with the rules and have the **same effect as rules**, to the extent not in conflict with the Income Tax Acts/rules or later guidelines — covering definitions/interpretations, manner of application, and matters within the Commissioner's approval/discretion.
- (3) Minister may prescribe tax credits (classes of persons, calculation, amounts).
- (4) Minister may amend/repeal/substitute the Schedule by rules.
- (5) Rules for tax treatment of exclusive economic zone activities.

## 11. Individual/married-couple provisions in this part (minor for companies)
- **Art. 49**: married couple income charged in the name of the responsible spouse; joint and several responsibility; election effective minimum 5 years.
- **Art. 49A**: separate return election (from YA 2021); conditions; revocation locked out for the year plus 4 succeeding years of assessment.
- **Art. 50**: separate computation election for earned income of the non-responsible spouse.
- **Art. 50A**: pension arrears charged in the year to which they refer (pre-YA 1999 arrears charged in YA 1999).
- **Art. 57**: personal retirement scheme credit — lower of 15% of contributions/premiums or €150 (or as prescribed); non-refundable, no carry-forward.

### Item 4 — Remission for default
- Commissioner may remit part/all where the default was due to a **reasonable excuse** — but (a) insufficiency of funds and (b) reliance on another person (incl. dilatoriness/inaccuracy of the person relied upon) are NOT reasonable excuses.

### Items 5–7 — Additional tax for OMISSION
- **Item 5**: **1.5% per month** (or part month) of the **endangered tax**, from the month the tax on chargeable income for the year was due and payable until the month the omission is rectified by a further return (ITMA art. 13) or an assessment is made.
- **Item 6 — reduced rates** where the taxpayer self-corrects by further return **before being notified in writing (ITMA art. 13(7)) of an enquiry**:
  - (a) further return delivered **within 12 months of the tax return date**: additional tax **fully remitted**;
  - (b) otherwise: **0.1% per month** of the endangered tax.
  - After enquiry notification but **before assessment**: **0.75% per month**.
- **Item 7 — cap**: maximum additional tax for an omission is **60 times the applicable monthly rate** (i.e. max 90% at 1.5%/month; 6% at 0.1%; 45% at 0.75%).

### Item 8 — Remission for omission
- Commissioner may remit part/all where: (a) the person sought and relied on written advice of a **tax professional** on the matter and submits the original written advice **with the return by the tax return date**; or (b) the person proves the omission was not due to fraud, art, contrivance or gross or wilful neglect. The advice is not a binding interpretation; remission refusable if the advice is spurious/frivolous (reviewable by the Administrative Review Tribunal on appeal). Complexity of a transaction or of the law is NOT of itself a valid ground under (b).

### Item 9 — Interest
- Additional tax is **in addition to and without prejudice to interest under ITMA art. 44(2A)**, but **no interest is charged on the additional tax itself**.

### Items 10–12 — Special cases
- Item 10: person who files an ITMA art. 12 election declaration without qualifying is deemed to have omitted all income (except art. 12(2)(b) income) until a full correct return is filed.
- Item 11: late filing of an art. 12 election declaration = deemed default continuing until a return is furnished.
- Item 12: where the Commissioner has estimated tax under ITMA art. 31(3) and given an ITMA art. 13(7) enquiry notice, and the person then files a return: **additional tax for omission applies in addition to additional tax for default**; endangered tax = tax on total income (less credits) as declared in that return; where an assessment/additional assessment is then made under ITMA art. 31(5), declared tax is treated as zero for the endangered-tax computation (with the item 12(2) additional tax deducted from the item 12(3) amount).

### Item 13 — Commissioner's powers
- May remit any part/all of Schedule additional tax; may impose conditions (e.g. no further default/omission within a set period, payment of tax by a set time); infringement forfeits any additional (discretionary) reduction — treated as a suspension, not cancellation; conditions and forfeiture must be communicated in writing; the discretion under item 13 is **not questionable in any appeal**.
