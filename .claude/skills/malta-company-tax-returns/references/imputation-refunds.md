# Malta Full Imputation System, Tax Accounts and Shareholder Refunds — Company Return Handling (YA 2025 / YA 2026)

Verified 2026-07-09. Primary statutes: Income Tax Act (ITA) Cap. 123; Income Tax Management Act (ITMA) Cap. 372; Tax Accounts (Income Tax) Rules **S.L. 123.101** (L.N. 79/2008, as amended up to L.N. 194/2019 — NOT S.L. 123.108, which is the Montenegro DTA Order); NID Rules S.L. 123.176; Consolidated Group (Income Tax) Rules S.L. 123.189; Tax Refunds and Registration Procedure Rules S.L. 372.25; FITWI Regulations L.N. 188/2025; Pillar Two L.N. 32/2024 as amended by L.N. 48/2026.

---

## 1. The five tax accounts and allocation in the return

### 1.1 Framework and mandatory ordering
- Allocation order: **FTA → IPA → FIA → MTA → UA**. FTA before all others; IPA before FIA/MTA; UA is the pure residual and the **only account that can be negative**.
- Sanction: rule 7 S.L. 123.101 — a materially incorrect allocation lets the Commissioner treat the return as not complete in all material respects (art. 10(5) ITMA).

### 1.2 Final Tax Account (rule 3, S.L. 123.101)
Allocated to FTA: (a) profits taxed under investment income provisions (15% final WHT on bank interest etc.); (b) profits exempt under Maltese law where distribution is also exempt in shareholders' hands; (d) dividends received out of another company's FTA; (e) after-tax profits from art. 5A property transfers; (ee) petroleum profits (art. 23); (f) profits carrying no tax credit on distribution; (i) art. 27G transfers (from YA 2020); (j) exempt grants/subsidies; (k) rents taxed under arts. 31A–31D; FITWI-taxed profits (L.N. 188/2025); **110% of NID-relieved profits** (rule 3(2): 100% direct + 10% reallocation out of the source account).
- Tax-credit-relieved income (Micro Invest, Investment Aid, R&D etc.): FTA only up to YA 2014 — **from YA 2015 → MTA** (L.N. 183/2015 proviso).
- **From YA 2020 (L.N. 194/2019): art. 12(1)(u) participation-exempt profits are NO LONGER allocated to the FTA — they fall to the Untaxed Account.**

### 1.3 Immovable Property Account (rules 5–6, S.L. 123.101)
Taxed profits derived directly/indirectly from Malta immovable property, not in the FTA:
- Rule 5(3): (a) transfers of Malta property outside art. 5A; (b) rents, premiums, accommodation (incl. hotels/timeshare, apportioned by 7% VAT-rate accommodation sales); (b)(iii) services via cables/wires/pipes annexed to tenements; (c) brokerage, professional services, construction, project management, tradesmen's work on such property; (d) accommodation-related management services; (f) anything the Commissioner deems property-derived; (g) after-tax gains on shares in Malta property-owning entities.
- **Notional (gross) allocations**: (e)(i) interest/fees from loans financing Malta property; (e)(ii) insurance premiums on such property; (h) **deemed annual market rent of €250 per square metre per annum** on owned-and-used property (related-party >25% rules).
- Mechanics (rule 5(4)): allocate all accounts first, then transfer notional amounts to IPA **first out of MTA, then FIA**; shortfalls carry forward or shift to related companies' IPAs (rule 5(7), >50% test).
- **Rule 9 election**: irrevocable election not to operate FIA/MTA — everything to IPA; rules 5(3)(e)/(h) then disapplied.

### 1.4 Foreign Income Account (ITA art. 2, exhaustive)
To the extent resulting from income shown in the return: (a) foreign royalties; dividends, capital gains, interest, rents, income/gains from a participating holding or its disposal (other than in Malta-resident vehicles), other foreign investment income; (b) foreign profits of Malta banks/financial institutions (>95% non-resident deposits; from YA 2016 only if specifically empowered); (c) foreign PE/branch profits (arm's-length); (d) dividends out of another Malta company's FIA. CISs make no FIA allocations (art. 67A(1)). FIA is the gateway to the FRFTC.

### 1.5 MTA and UA
- **MTA**: all taxed profits not in FIA (and not FTA/IPA) — typically Malta trading income at 35%; plus tax-credit-relieved income from YA 2015.
- **UA**: residual (can be negative); disallowed-expenditure differences, timing differences, and (from YA 2020) participation-exempt income.

### 1.6 Reporting in the company return (TA2 e-CO)
- **Page 3**: accounting-profit adjustment split into three columns (IPA / MTA / FIA); **pages 4–5**: self-assessment per taxed-account column (capital allowances, account-specific loss carry-forwards/group relief retaining account character, NID, 35% tax, credits, DTR/FRFTC per account); **page 6**: Record of Reserves and Distributions — brought-forward / allocated / distributed / carried-forward per FTA, IPA, MTA, FIA, UA, reconciling to **TIFD code 3950 "Total Reserves"**.
- Key TRA schedules: **TRA 08** dividends received (gross split per account per payer, Malta tax, DTR/FRFTC — shows the "25% Element Grossed Up" for FRFTC); **TRA 09** interest; **TRA 11** rents; **TRA 18** exempt income (allocation FTA vs UA, legislation-referenced); **TRA 30** double taxation relief (type / taxed account / amount); **TRA 35** tax credits utilised; **TRA 61** FTA movement; **TRA 62/62A** IPA first and notional allocations + rule 9 election questions; **TRA 63_1/63_2** FIA and MTA analysis by category and tax rate; **TRA 100** NID.

### 1.7 Distribution consequences per account
- Full imputation: company deducts tax at the rate borne on the underlying profits from any dividend (other than UA dividends); shareholder gets full credit (ITA art. 59(1)(a)).
- **FTA dividends**: no further tax, not chargeable income of any person, **no credit or refund** (art. 68(1)(c)).
- **UA dividends**: 15% withholding when paid to a "recipient" (Malta-resident non-company persons, certain trustees/EU-EEA individuals) (arts. 61–66).
- **Ordering on distribution**: dividends deemed paid out of **IPA before MTA** (art. 68(3)); refund-registered shareholders deemed to receive MTA/FIA profits pro-rata (art. 68(5)) — articles directing them to MTA/FIA-only dividends are disregarded.
- Refunds attach **only to MTA and FIA** distributions; never FTA/IPA/UA.

## 2. Shareholder refunds — ITMA art. 48(4)/(4A)

| Refund | Base | Conditions | Accounts | Effective Malta rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **6/7** | Advance Company Income Tax (ACIT) | Default for trading profits; NOT for passive interest/royalties; NOT where DTR claimed on FIA profits; NOT on immovable-property profits | MTA, FIA | ~5% |
| **5/7** | ACIT | Passive interest/royalties, or participating-holding dividends failing art. 12(1)(u) anti-abuse | MTA, FIA | ~10% |
| **2/3** | "Malta tax paid" | Any DTR claimed on FIA profits (mandatory route — bars 6/7 and 5/7) | FIA | 6.25%–11.67% |
| **100%** | All Malta tax paid | FIA profits from participating holding or its disposal; dividends must satisfy anti-abuse conditions | FIA | 0% |

- **ACIT** (ITMA 42B(2)): tax deductible under ITA art. 59 on the FIA/MTA distribution, computed **before** deducting treaty/Commonwealth/unilateral relief credits (arts. 74(a)–(c)) but **net of the FRFTC** (art. 74(d)).
- **"Passive interest or royalties"** (ITA art. 2): interest/royalty income not derived directly or indirectly from a trade or business AND which suffered foreign tax at a rate **below 5%**. Foreign tax ≥5% or trade-derived → 6/7 applies (subject to the DTR rule).
- **2/3 computation** (ITMA 48(4)(c)): "Malta tax paid" = tax actually paid PLUS the reduction from treaty/unilateral/Commonwealth relief — so with treaty/unilateral relief the 2/3 is of the full 35% charge, **capped at Malta tax actually paid**; with **FRFTC**, 2/3 is of Malta tax only (35% less FRFTC).
- **100% refund** (48(4)(b)): elective alternative to the participation exemption — declare and tax at 35%, full refund on distribution; for dividend-derived profits the same anti-abuse conditions as ITA 12(1)(u) are replicated verbatim in 48(4)(b). Where dividends fail the anti-abuse tests, the 100% refund is NOT available; the 5/7 refund applies.
- **Cap on all refunds**: never exceed tax actually paid to the Commissioner on the distributed profits.

### 2.1 Registration and procedure
- **Shareholder registration** with the Commissioner (S.L. 372.25), one-time, before the company's first tax payment deadline; e-form requires activity, accounting period, capital structure/currency, direct shareholders and **ultimate individual beneficial owners**; filed by the registered tax representative; BO disclosure via the MTCA online portal (since 2020).
- **DDT10 is distinct from refund registration**: it is the art. 47(3)(e) Duty on Documents and Transfers Act determination (>90% of business interests outside Malta) giving stamp-duty exemption and extending the company's tax payment deadline from 9 to **18 months** after year-end; 3-year validity, renewed via the online DDT10 service.
- **Refund preconditions**: actual dividend distribution + **dividend certificate under ITA art. 59(5)** (per taxed account: gross profits, Malta tax after credits, treaty/unilateral relief, FRFTC set-off, other credits, net dividend); company return with audited accounts filed complete and correct; company tax paid; **claim within 4 years (ITMA 48(5))**; refund payable **not later than the 14th day after it falls due** (48(8)); paid in the **currency of the company's share capital** (ITMA 31A); the refund itself is **not taxable** (48(6)); wrongful claim penalty = amount claimed plus repayment with 7%/month additional tax (48(10)).
- **Eligibility**: both resident and non-resident registered shareholders qualify; economics deter Malta-resident individuals (net dividend + refund both taxed → ~38.25% total), so claims are in practice by non-residents or Malta holdcos with non-resident UBOs.

## 3. Double taxation relief in the return

### 3.1 Four reliefs, strict order (ITA arts. 74–75) — never combined on the same income
1. **Treaty relief** (arts. 76–78); 2. **Unilateral relief** (arts. 79–88) only if no treaty/Commonwealth relief; 3. **Commonwealth relief** (art. 89, largely obsolete); 4. **FRFTC** (arts. 92–95) only where none of the above is available.

### 3.2 Treaty relief
Ordinary credit: gross up foreign income for foreign tax; credit = lower of Malta tax on that income and foreign tax paid; claim within 2 years after end of the relevant YA; no refundable excess. Example: €13,500 net of 10% Italian tax → gross €15,000, Malta 35% = €5,250, credit €1,500, payable €3,750.

### 3.3 Unilateral relief
Same mechanics for non-treaty countries; extends to underlying tax on dividends (≥10% voting chain, art. 82); **art. 88 proof requirement** — claimant must prove the income bore foreign tax and its amount.

### 3.4 FRFTC (arts. 92–95)
- Only for **companies** on income they are **specifically empowered to receive** which falls to be allocated to the **FIA** (excluding FIA dividends from another Malta company); documentary evidence = **certificate by a certified public accountant and auditor** (art. 92(1)(c)).
- Credit = **25% of the amount receivable** after actual foreign tax but before other deductions; the credit is **added to the income (gross-up)**; capped at **85% of the Malta tax payable** on the FIA profits (after other DTR set-offs); no refund of excess; art. 95 anti-avoidance.
- Standard computation: net 100 → gross 125 → tax 43.75 → credit 25 → Malta tax 18.75 (**18.75%**); after 2/3 refund (12.5) → **6.25% combined**. With expenses the 85% cap can bite and leakage can fall to ~1.4%.

### 3.5 In the return
Foreign income reported grossed-up on page 4 (TRA 08/09); relief claimed on page 5 per taxed-account column; **TRA 30** summarises DTR by type/account/amount; evidence retained: foreign tax proof (art. 88) or auditor's FIA certificate (FRFTC).

## 4. Participation exemption (ITA art. 12(1)(u); art. 2 "participating holding")

### 4.1 Participating holding — equity holding (≥2 of 3 rights: votes / profits / winding-up assets; not a property company) + ANY ONE of:
| # | Condition |
|---|---|
| (a) | Direct holding of **≥5% of equity shares** conferring ≥5% of any two of the three rights |
| (b) | Option to acquire the **entire balance** of the equity shares |
| (c) | **Right of first refusal** on disposal/redemption/cancellation of the other equity shares |
| (d) | Right to **sit on the board or appoint a director** |
| (e) | Investment of **≥ €1,164,000** (acquisition value), held uninterruptedly **≥183 days** |
| (f) | Holding for the **furtherance of the company's own business**, not trading stock |

Extends to holdings in non-electing partnerships/EEIGs, equivalent foreign bodies, and non-resident limited-liability CIVs.

### 4.2 Anti-abuse — DIVIDENDS only
Exemption for participating-holding dividends applies only if the body satisfies **any one** of: (i) resident/incorporated in an EU country; (ii) subject to any foreign tax **≥15%**; (iii) **≤50%** of its income from passive interest or royalties; failing all three, **both**: not a portfolio investment AND foreign tax **≥5%**.

### 4.3 Gains
Gains on transfer of a participating holding are exempt **without** the anti-abuse conditions. Caveats: transfers of holdings in Malta-resident vehicles qualify only via the art. 12(1)(c)(ii) beneficial-owner test with clawback; property-company carve-out at the equity-holding level.

### 4.4 Election to tax; anti-hybrid; non-cooperative jurisdictions
- Exemption claimed by **not showing the income/gain in the return**; declaring it = election to tax at 35%, with the shareholder's **100% refund** on distribution under ITMA 48(4)(b).
- **Anti-hybrid (PSD, from 1 Jan 2016)**: exemption denied to the extent the distribution is **deductible for the paying EU subsidiary**.
- **EU non-cooperative jurisdiction exclusion**: no exemption where the body is resident in a listed jurisdiction ≥3 months of the basis year, unless significant people functions there are proven.
- Return mechanics: exempt income in **TRA 18**; since YA 2020 exempt 12(1)(u) profits → **Untaxed Account** (previously FTA); taxed participating-holding income → **FIA** (100%-refund route).

## 5. Notional Interest Deduction (S.L. 123.176; ITA art. 14(1)(o); MTCA Guidelines v1.1, 20 Dec 2023)

- **Formula**: NID = reference rate × risk capital (year-end of basis year). **Reference rate = yield to maturity on Malta Government Stocks with remaining term ~20 years + 5% premium**; use the CBM quarterly "Indicative Rates based on Malta Government Benchmark Stocks" (Year-20 row) at the quarter-end coinciding with (or immediately preceding) the accounting period end.
- **Rates** (computed from CBM Year-20 yields — verify current quarter at centralbankmalta.org/malta-government-stocks): YA 2024 (Q4 2023) 3.61% → **8.61%**; YA 2025 (Q4 2024) 3.70% → **8.70%**; YA 2026 (Q4 2025) 4.19% → **9.19%**; Q1 2026 4.40% → 9.40%; Q2 2026 4.16% → 9.16% (non-December year-ends). MTCA publishes the method, not a rate table.
- **Risk capital**: share/partnership capital, share premium, positive retained earnings, interest-free loans/debt, contribution reserves, any other positive equity balance; for non-residents, the part attributable to the Malta PE. Reduced by risk capital not employed in producing income or employed in producing **exempt** income.
- **Cap and carry-forward**: NID ≤ **90% of chargeable income before NID**; deductible only against profits standing to be allocated to **MTA or FIA**; excess carried forward **indefinitely**; remaining ≥10% taxed at 35%; no double deduction.
- **Deemed income rule** (r. 5): shareholders/partners at end of the basis year deemed to receive **interest income equal to the NID claimed**, pro-rata to nominal risk-capital value; investment-income provisions do not apply; **non-resident shareholders generally exempt** under ITA art. 12(1)(c)(i) (no Malta ownership/control, no Malta PE).
- **Tax accounts**: **110% of the NID-relieved profits allocated to the FTA** — no further tax and **no refund** on distribution, so NID replaces the refund system for those profits.
- **Election/claim**: optional, annually, **in the return** (full or partial; unclaimed NID for a year is forfeited — only the >90% excess carries forward); requires demonstrated **approval of ALL shareholders/partners** (those as at end of the preceding basis year; needed where the claim actually reduces total income). Claimed on return page 4 with attachment **TRA 100**, driving FTA reallocation lines in TRA 61.
- **Anti-avoidance** (r. 6): Commissioner nullifies undue advantages from arrangements duplicating/cascading risk capital among >50% commonly owned persons; art. 51 GAAR preserved.

## 6. Fiscal units — Consolidated Group (Income Tax) Rules (S.L. 123.189, from YA 2020)

- **Eligibility**: parent holds in a "95% subsidiary" **any two of three**: ≥95% voting rights; ≥95% entitlement to distributable profits; ≥95% entitlement to winding-up assets (tested at end of the year preceding the YA of election); **coterminous accounting periods** (same start and end); no company in more than one fiscal unit; excluded: securitisation vehicles, finance-leasing companies; **tax-compliance gate** — no outstanding income tax/VAT/FSS balances or filings.
- **Effect**: principal taxpayer files **one consolidated return**; transparent subsidiaries exempt from filing; intra-unit transactions deemed not to have occurred (except art. 5A property transfers, property-company share transfers, dividends from pre-consolidation taxed profits); income attributed to the principal retaining source/character/tax-account allocation; non-resident subsidiary treated as foreign PE.
- **~5% upfront** (Rule 6(2)): where shareholders would be refund-entitled, the unit's chargeable income is taxed at 35% minus the refund-equivalent — e.g. 6/7 refund → **5% effective, paid upfront, no refund claim or wait**; no art. 48(4)/(4A) refunds thereafter (Rule 6(3)).
- **Anti-abuse (Rule 13)**: if unit tax < 95% of aggregate standalone tax (post refunds/credits), the shortfall is grossed into a deemed untaxed-account dividend taxed at 35%, payable within 14 days after the return date.
- **Registration**: via the principal's MTCA portal profile (same registered tax representative for all members); **election window = 6 months starting the day after the financial period end, not earlier than 1 August** — for 31 Dec year-ends: **1 January–30 June** of the following year; same window for composition changes; late election shifts effect to the following YA; **minority shareholder approval required** where the subsidiary is <100% owned.
- **Compliance**: **audited consolidated financial statements** of the fiscal unit mandatory (with disclosed simplifications where prepared solely for Rule 11); **joint and several liability** of principal and its 100% transparent subsidiaries; normal corporate return deadlines + e-filing extensions; **18-month payment deadline only if ALL members hold a DDT10 determination or are non-resident non-taxable** — otherwise tax due by the return date; FIA-allocated tax due within 18 months in any case; VAT/FSS stay per-member.
- **Exit**: loss of 95% status or coterminous year-end → out from start of that basis year; voluntary exit requires prior settlement of unit tax; losses/tax-account balances remain with the principal.

## 7. Pillar Two in Malta (position as at mid-2026)

- **Transposition**: L.N. 32/2024 — skeletal (Chapters I, VIII, IX, X of Directive (EU) 2022/2523 only); **no IIR or UTPR charging provisions enacted**.
- **Article 50 deferral**: Malta elected not to apply IIR/UTPR for **six consecutive fiscal years from 31 Dec 2023** (to ~2030); Malta's UTPR percentage deemed zero; in-scope Malta UPEs picked up by another Member State's IIR.
- **QDMTT**: none for 2024–2026; postponed until end-2029.
- **FITWI 15% elective regime** (Art. 22B ITA; L.N. 188/2025, from **YA 2025**): elective **15% final tax without imputation** — no credit, no shareholder refunds; 5-consecutive-YA lock-in (and 5-year lock-out after reverting); liability floored at the standard-system outcome; FITWI profits → **FTA**. NOT a QDMTT; a 15% Malta bill does not guarantee a 15% GloBE ETR.
- **Obligations of Malta constituent entities of in-scope groups** (€750m consolidated revenue in ≥2 of 4 preceding years): **no local GIR/TTIR filing** in Malta during the deferral (filed by UPE/designated entity abroad; first GIR for calendar-FY2024 groups due **30 June 2026**); the notification obligation to the CfTC was **abolished by L.N. 48/2026**; entities must still supply GloBE data to the group's filing entity.
- **Preparer watch-points**: Malta's shareholder refunds are **disqualified refundable imputation taxes** under GloBE — excluded from covered taxes, so a group's Malta GloBE ETR computes at ~5%, not 35%, creating top-up exposure under other jurisdictions' IIR; model refund claims / fiscal-unit consolidation against foreign top-up clawback; consider the FITWI election (designed to count fully as covered tax); check Transitional CbCR Safe Harbour eligibility.

## Reconciliation notes
- The Tax Accounts (Income Tax) Rules are **S.L. 123.101** — commonly miscited as S.L. 123.108 (the Montenegro DTA Order).
- Participation-exempt (12(1)(u)) profits: **Untaxed Account since YA 2020** (L.N. 194/2019); pre-2020 material shows the old FTA allocation.
- Where participating-holding dividends fail the anti-abuse tests, the **100% refund is not available** (48(4)(b) replicates the tests); the 5/7 refund applies — some advisory pages state otherwise, contradicted by the statute.
